## Key facts (2026-07-04 deep dive)

- **Master source**: Jay's own self-sent "art resume" email (Gmail, 2024-08-12): *"Flaming Lotus Girls (welding, fabrication and fire safety, 2009-2011)."* A near-identical line in the 2026 Church of Launder Day Saints proposal bio expands the skill list slightly: *"Flaming Lotus Girls (combustion design, controls, welding, fabrication, fire safety and fire marshal approvals)."*
- **FLG itself** is a real, well-documented, long-running (since 2000) Bay Area fire-art collective known for large-scale Burning Man sculptures (e.g., Serpent Mother, Angel of the Apocalypse, Soma) — has its own Wikipedia page and extensive press history.
- **No specific named FLG sculpture/build was found tied to Jay** in Gmail or Drive — her documented FLG presence is mostly as a **Flaming Lotus Girls mailing list member** (`flaminglotus@yahoogroups.com`) cross-posting into the broader Shipyard community in 2009-2010, consistent with her being embedded in the same maker/fire-art social network (Shipyard, APL, FLG, and later Le Attrata/Therm all overlap heavily in this era — see `../all-power-labs/README.md`).
- **"Fishbug" clarification — important correction.** Jay described a project she calls "fish bug" as Flaming Lotus Girls-adjacent. Research found a real, independently well-documented Burning Man 2009 sculpture called **"Fishbug"** (full name **"Chimera Sententia"**) — a 25-foot-long, 15-foot-high steel kinetic fire-breathing creature with a translucent fiberglass head. **It was built by Rebecca Anders and Jess Hobbs at The Shipyard — NOT by Flaming Lotus Girls.** Jay was cc'd on the `fishbug@fishbug.net` mailing list in 2010 ("Re: [basgap] Shipyard left wide open") and was an active Shipyard community member during exactly the Fishbug build/life period (2009-2011) — but no email or Drive document assigns her a specific named role (no welding logs, no safety docs) on the Fishbug build itself. **Her involvement appears to be general Shipyard-community overlap, not a documented hands-on build role** — worth asking her directly whether "fish bug" refers to actual build work on Chimera Sententia, or just being part of the same community/era.
- Sources for Fishbug/Chimera Sententia (all independent of Jay): [Dust to Ashes — Chimera Sententia Fishbug](https://dusttoashes.com/all-burning-man-photos/chimera-sententia-fishbug/), [Flux Foundation — Fishbug](https://www.fluxfoundation.org/fishbug/), [Rebecca Anders portfolio — Fishbug](https://www.rebecca-anders.com/ls-portfolio/fishbug), [Burning Man 2009 Art Installations](https://burningman.org/about/history/brc-history/event-archives/2009-2/2009-art-installations/).
