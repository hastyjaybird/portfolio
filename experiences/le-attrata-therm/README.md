## Key facts (2026-07-04 deep dive)

- **Spelling correction: the correct name is "Le Attrata"** (not "La Attrada" or "La Atrada" — those are phonetic approximations Jay used). All primary sources (Kickstarter, press, Burning Man gallery, crew credit photo) spell it **Le Attrata**.
- **What it is**: a 40'×40'×20' kinetic fire-and-light installation — three 12-foot-wingspan stainless-steel moths perched on 18-foot crystalline spires. Built by **Therm**, a West Oakland fire-art collective fronted by **Justin Gray**, operating out of a shop and later organized as "Therm LLC." Lead artists: **Margaret Long** (originated the idea) and **Orion Fredericks** (co-lead), with a ~30-person crew.
- **Debut/tour**: Burning Man 2016 (Kickstarter-funded: "Le Attrata, Fire and Technology in Sculpture by Therm"); Bay Area Maker Faire 2017; exhibited at **Chatsworth House, UK** (at Burning Man's invitation); returned to storage at **Nimby Doyle**, Emeryville.
- **Jay's role, per her own 2024 art resume**: *"La Attrata (30ft fire sculpture, welding, combustion dynamics, electronics and controls, Burningman 2016)."* This covers welding, the combustion/flame-effects system, and electronics/controls (which covers LED addressing) — fire safety itself is attributed separately to her earlier Flaming Lotus Girls stint, not this project specifically.
- **Confirmed crew member**: appears on two official credit/crew photos in Google Drive (folder `la_attrada_credit`) — a plaque/poster listing ~30 names under "LE ATTRATA / Lead Artists: Margaret Long, Orion Fredericks," including **"Julia Hasty"** (OCR misread as "Joe Hasty" in one pass) among ~27 other crew names. Plaque text lists the collective's combined skills: *"metal fabrication, machining, turbine retro-fitting, LED programming, 3-D printing, textile experimentation, wood-working, budget management, spreadsheet wizardry, electronics mastery, solder monkeys, sewing, human resource management, combustion editing, load mastering, rigging, design, engineering."*
- **Jay went by "Jay Bird" / gaia.revolts@gmail.com** in the Therm/Le Attrata community — this alias is the most active account in that circle's thread history (2016-2024).
- **Documented hands-on contribution**: she offered to **waterjet-cut 3in×3in stainless steel pendants** from a DXF file for the project (thread "logo help," March 2016) — a real CNC/fabrication contribution. She was active enough in the group to request Burning Man tickets via the group's Google Group and make plans around show logistics (Maker Faire 2017, a "Precompression" 2017 event offer).
- **No granular technical documents found** (no wiring diagrams, propane manifold schematics, or written safety protocols in her own hand) — the technical depth described below comes from press/Kickstarter coverage, not from a personal engineering writeup she authored.
- **Technical specs of the sculpture** (for context, from Kickstarter/press): turbine-driven blast furnaces (retrofitted turbines, back-driven with electric motors instead of diesel) producing ignite/spark/pop/whine flame effects; three human "operators" play the moths like instruments from control podiums; ~80 gallons of fuel over 4 hours of typical show time, 20-minute cycles with cooling breaks; **over 2,000 programmable LEDs controlled via Raspberry Pi 3 + FadeCandy**, including a hexagonal light well of 3D-printed illuminated crystals; 60% of materials salvaged; an internal stress test nicknamed the "Angry Manchild" test. Sources: [Kickstarter](https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/1530767649/le-attrata), [Vice — "This Flaming Insect Sculpture is a Marvel in Engineering"](https://vice.com/en_uk/article/bmyq75/flaming-sculpture-festival-marvel-engineering), [Burning Man Gallery](https://gallery.burningman.org/asset/7556b3a5-2739-41c8-906a-a8f8def27cea).
- **A real, somewhat bitter authorship dispute (2024) — sensitive, handle carefully.** In late Aug-Oct 2024, Orion Fredericks and Jennifer Elliot revived "Therm LLC" and moved to claim sole ownership/artist credit for Le Attrata, rejecting Margaret Long's offer of shared ownership; Margaret ultimately stood down rather than fund an ~$80k legal fight. Jay ("Jay Bird") was a direct participant in the group thread discussing this: *"Thanks for the update. That all feels off. Removing the plaque would be a real shitty move."* Another crew member, Matthew Andreoli, responded: *"Yet not surprising, if he is claiming to have done all the electrical and machining. Sad to see."* — i.e., **credit for the electronics/controls work Jay describes doing is explicitly contested by Orion's camp.** The crew-name plaque was reportedly still on the sculpture as of the dispute (per Char Zvolanek). **This is genuinely sensitive — Jay should decide how (or whether) to reference this dispute in any public-facing bio, since it involves a live disagreement over credit among named real people.**
