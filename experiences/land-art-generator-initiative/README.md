## Key facts (verified, 2026-07-03)

- **LAGI 2020 Fly Ranch** was a renewable-energy public-art design challenge held in partnership with the **Burning Man Project**, sited at Fly Ranch — Burning Man's permanent land holding in Nevada.
- **Jay served as a Technical Advisor and Shortlisting Committee juror** ("Group 1"), reviewing submitted design proposals from a technical feasibility standpoint alongside a roster of engineers, architects, and renewable-energy specialists.
- **Real named contacts confirmed:** Matt Sundquist and several other Burning Man Project staff handled logistics on the funding/land-partner side (LAGI itself ran the technical judging).
- **Timeline:** invited ~Nov 2020, technical review November–December 2020, shortlisting committee meetings January 2021, results announced March 10, 2021.
- **No contract, invoice, or payment record found anywhere** for this engagement — unlike Resilient Markets' genuine paying clients (MaxETA, Sol Solutions). All correspondence reads as a volunteer/juror invitation ("thank you for dedicating your time," "thank you for volunteering to participate"), the same pattern previously identified for Max Energy Lab (see `../max-energy-lab/README.md`).
- **Recruitment origin, confirmed 2026-07-05:** a prior contact from Jay's network mentioned meeting at a past event where the LAGI 2020 Fly Ranch design challenge was first announced — a personal-network referral, not a cold invitation. **Keep Burning Man references minimal in any front-facing material (per Jay, 2026-07-04) — this is a values-driven, one-off volunteer-judging credential, not a signal that Jay identifies with festival/Burner culture.**
- **What LAGI actually is (web-researched 2026-07-04):** founded in 2008 by architect Robert Ferry and artist Elizabeth Monoian, on the premise that renewable energy infrastructure doesn't have to be hidden in a substation or a field — it can be public art that people want to spend time near, that teaches them something, and that shifts how a whole community relates to the energy transition. Since 2010, LAGI's biennial international design competitions (Dubai, Abu Dhabi, New York City, Copenhagen, Santa Monica, Melbourne) have drawn over 1,000 submitted designs from 80+ countries. The Fly Ranch challenge Jay judged wasn't a paper exercise: the ten shortlisted teams split a real $150,000 in prototype-construction grants to build functioning, full-scale installations on site.

It's a concrete data point that technical rigor and creative/cultural work aren't in tension for her — they're two different levers on the same problem, and she'll put her own expertise behind the second one, unpaid, when she believes in it. It doesn't carry the weight of a client relationship, but it says something real about which invitations she says yes to without a fee attached.
