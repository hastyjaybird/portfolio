## Why this is its own folder

Per Jay's explicit clarification (2026-07-03): 10Power deserves its own folder, separate from Resilient Markets, because the payment and work relationship is genuinely direct — Sandra/10Power pays Jay personally for 10Power work, which is structurally distinct from Resilient Markets client engagements (where RM itself is the contracting party and takes its 15% admin cut).

## Key facts (verified, 2026-07-03)

- **The Jay/Sandra relationship predates any business arrangement by years — a real, close personal friendship.** Gmail shows the two were already close by 2016 (a "one more time" thread about borrowed solar panels and a bikini top left at Sandra's place, tied to a Burning Man trip), and the two eventually became literal housemates — a shared household with Mario Landau-Holdsworth, Leif/Horst Bansner, and Sydney, complete with recurring "house dinner" scheduling threads (2020–2022).
- **Real direct 10Power project work found, predating and outside of Resilient Markets:**
  - **2018: PR cooperative-formation project.** Jay personally set up and administered a HubSpot CRM for tracking cooperative-formation leads/contacts, working alongside Sandra/10Power, David Geeter, Tom (xrivercap.com), and Fundación Borincana contacts — a direct technical/operational contribution to a joint 10Power-adjacent Puerto Rico cooperative effort.
  - **2019: Introduced Sandra/10Power to Matt Wiggins (Remedy Ventures — the same financier behind IOGS/Guardiola Real Estate, see `../iogs/README.md`)** for a ~$7.5M capital raise — a direct instance of Jay's Puerto Rico investor network being deployed on 10Power's behalf.
  - **2019: Direct collaboration with Environmental Defense Fund (EDF)** on Puerto Rico community-solar-microgrid site-selection criteria, with Sandra explicitly connecting the PR site-selection challenge to parallel work the World Bank was struggling with in Haiti.
  - **Ongoing informal technical thought-partnership**, 2016–2019: Sandra regularly ran new technology ideas by Jay (pyrolysis/tire-recycling technology, an "Africa" biomass project, a landfill-gas team introduction via the Hult Prize) — a genuine, sustained technical-advisor relationship independent of any specific paid project.
- **The actual origin of what became Resilient Markets is a 10Power-adjacent email, not the LLC filing itself:** on April 6, 2020, Sandra sent a "Resilient Markets Consulting Consortium" email introducing Jay, Mario, Mimi Zemmelman, Brent Schulkin, Kelly Erhart, and **Jeff (jeff@10pwr.com, a 10Power team member)** as "Badass Humans," proposing they combine "superpowers" — this is the actual founding conversation, about six weeks before the formal LegalZoom LLC filing began in May 2020 (see `../resilient-markets-maxeta/README.md`).
- **10Power pays Jay directly, confirmed 2026:** a Feb 2026 email from Sandra ("Your 10Power 2025 Tax Documents") was sent directly to Jay's personal and Resilient Markets emails, separate from any Resilient Markets LLC tax filing — confirming 10Power-specific income is handled as a direct payment relationship, exactly as Jay described (to avoid an unnecessary 15% RM admin cut on money that never touches RM).
- **Flagship, most-developed direct project: the Eklutna Energy Plan** (Native Village of Eklutna, Alaska) — see its own dedicated folder, `../eklutna-10power/README.md`, for full detail (real $50K TSAF grant, ongoing NREL collaboration, active through late 2025).
- **10Power's own flagship mission is Haiti** (UNICEF HQ solar+storage, desalination for ~40,000 people, women-in-solar workforce program) — run directly by Sandra; **Jay has not worked on 10Power's Haiti projects.**

## Summary — why this is compelling

The 10Power relationship shows how a deep personal friendship, not a cold business connection, became one of Jay's most durable and technically substantive professional relationships — she and Sandra were housemates and close friends years before any of this became a paying arrangement, and the eventual Resilient Markets partnership itself grew directly out of a "let's combine our superpowers" email Sandra sent as a friend, not a formal business proposal. The direct-payment structure (10Power pays Jay personally, bypassing Resilient Markets' own admin fee) is also a small but telling detail about how the two of them think about efficiency and fairness between projects — routing money the simplest way rather than the most bureaucratically "correct" one, because they trust each other enough not to need the extra structure.
