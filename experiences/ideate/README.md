## Key facts (2026-07-05 deep dive)

- **Founding**: IDEATE was founded ~2011 ("10 years ago" per a May 2021 nonprofit-launch email). No single founder's name is documented; described by longtime mentor Herman Gyr as created by "a group of spectacular humans." The earliest concrete planning document found is an April 29, 2015 "Core Team Meeting" doc titled "Bringing more Sustainability to Burning Man and more Burning Man to the world!" — a mission-refounding moment, not the literal 2011 founding.
- **Jay's documented role was specifically power/solar, not general camp leadership:**
  - 2015: listed under Solar build and bike racks, and "Moop SUPERSTARS of Strike" on the team-planning spreadsheet.
  - 2016: listed as Power team member and Clean/Strike Lead; actively coordinated the solar 1-pager and wiring diagram with Daniel Barcay, Ben Kimmich, and Evan Steiner. A camp-org email states: "I'll defer to Jaybird on specifics, as she is leading the solar charge, with meaningful support from Daniel."
  - 2017: promoted to **Power team Lead** on IDEATE's official "Humans Organization" roster, alongside "Liptonite."
- **The composting origin story — `[INTERVIEW-DEPTH STORY]`, confirmed via a 2017 article Sandra Kwak wrote for the camp's own site:** in 2015, Jay and Sandra Kwak ("Ra") personally drove IDEATE's own camp compost off the playa in a "hot wired box truck full of compost, recycling, tiny homes and bikes" (Jay hotwired the truck's starter herself — "with her PhD sexy scientist skills," per Sandra's own words) to **Full Circle Compost**, a composting/horse-ranch operation run by inmates at the **Northern Nevada Correctional Center**, meeting "Compost King" Craig Witt. This was IDEATE's own camp-level compost disposal pipeline — a real, hands-on solution Jay helped personally execute.
- **The playa-wide scaling — confirmed by Jay (2026-07-05): she was part of that year's compost program and was "the main lift" on making it successful, though it was a team effort.** Jay personally drove the hotwired truck with Sandra Kwak to the high-security prison that year, and considers herself a driving force behind the citywide composting project's success — not just the earlier camp-level origin. A camp member named "Magnolia" is also credited in the record for scaling it playa-wide the same/following year — treat this as a real team effort with Jay as a lead contributor, per her own account, rather than crediting the scaling to one single person.
- The compost program continued for years as the "Burning Man Compost Project"/"Black Rock Compost Project," with satellite camps contributing ~$4/person, 3 "Compostainers" by 2018, and a "Compostifesto" manifesto. By 2021, when IDEATE became a formal 501(c)3 nonprofit ("IDEATE Community," EIN 85-0923617, under Executive Director Kevin Blake), **the Compost Project became the nonprofit's flagship focus** — "prototyping and expanding it into the real world" (off-playa).
- **Burning Man's own citywide sustainability plan — confirmed by Jay (2026-07-05): she did NOT help with this.** That workstream was owned by Amanda Ravenhill and Ryan Kushner, not Jay — drop any "helped build Burning Man's sustainability plan" framing entirely. **The real, confirmed citywide contribution is the composting project**, not the sustainability-plan advocacy.
- **Note: DPW Power (a separate, one-year volunteer stint with Burning Man's own Department of Public Works, alongside "Monk"/Ricardo Romero Gianoli) is not part of IDEATE** — per Jay's clarification (2026-07-05), these were two separate Burning Man-related threads. See `../burning-man/README.md` (umbrella) and `../burning-man-solar/README.md` for the full DPW Power detail.
- **A real governance crisis, June 2018:** a formal "IDEATE Quorum" of ~33 long-tenured members (camped 2015–2017) was convened to vote Go/White-Label/No-Go on whether the camp would continue, following unspecified internal disagreement. The camp did continue. Jay ("Jaybird Hasty") is listed among the qualifying long-term members in this vote, with no distinguishing role noted beyond that.
- **Continuity**: IDEATE ran in-person at least 2015–2018, went virtual during the 2020 BRC cancellation ("IDEATE live @ BRCvr," an AltSpace VR event), became a 501(c)3 nonprofit in 2021, and the camp/community continued at least through 2023 (a July 2023 fundraiser email).
- Other named core members/leads across years: Herman Gyr, Sandra Kwak ("Ra," later 10Power founder/CEO — see `../10power/README.md`), Amanda Ravenhill, Ryan Kushner, Daniel Barcay, Evan Steiner, Ben Kimmich, Julian Nadel, Kat/Kathleen Stansell (Nadel), Ilana Lipsett, Greg Delaune, Tim West, Chris West, Antonio Sala, Satya Kamdar, Magnolia, Kevin Blake.
