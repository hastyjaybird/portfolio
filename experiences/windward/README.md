## Origin story `[INTERVIEW-DEPTH STORY]` (2026-07-04, per Jay)

Jay met Walt Patrick at an Earth Day festival that got rained out — only the truly committed people stuck around, and she was one of them. Walt turned out to be an old chemist himself, and he already knew about gasifiers well before Jim Mason or APL existed; he needed help building gasifiers at Windward's educational farm. He was also genuinely excited about gas-to-liquids (GTL) conversion, and it was Walt who first sparked her interest in that specific field. **Walt is also the one who first told her about Devinder Mahajan** — the initial spark that eventually led her, years later, to deep-dive into Mahajan's patents, consider him a GTL "rockstar," and get recruited into his Stony Brook PhD program (see `../phd-stony-brook/README.md` for that full arc).

The real draw to Windward, in her own words, was finding "a hard tech sustainability nerd like myself" — specifically drawn to the Biomass2Methanol project itself, not the broader intentional-community lifestyle. Outside of her American Red Cross and Portland Biodiesel jobs, **this was her first hands-on work with gasification** — meaning the entire arc that follows (APL, the Shipyard, her PhD under Mahajan) traces back to this one rained-out Earth Day festival and an old chemist who happened to know about gasifiers before almost anyone else did.

## Key facts (verified, 2026-07-04)

- **Windward is a real, still-operating nonprofit**, founded as an intentional community that relocated to Klickitat County, WA in 1987–1988; functions as "an education and research lab for a variety of sustainable projects," with a full-time internship program drawing under- and post-graduate students. Still active today at windward.org.
- **Date range resolved:** multiple conflicting ranges existed across old resume drafts and Drive files — "2007–2008," "Jul–Aug 2007 (one month)," "04/07 to Present" (2008-era CV), "April 2007 to current" (an older draft), "December 2007 to current" (an even earlier/rougher draft, using the name "Windward Foundation"), and "May 2007 – June 2008." The one-month figure isn't supported by anything found and appears to be an erroneous fragment. **May 2007 – June 2008 (~14 months) is the best-supported range** — it's the one used consistently across both of her most polished, professionally-formatted resumes (2015 and 2017/2018), superseding the rougher/earlier draft variants.
- **Actual role/title — "Lead Researcher."** Her professional CVs list this as her Windward title. Earlier/rougher drafts also used "Project Lead and Chemist" and "Lead Chemist." She co-taught interns alongside Director Walt Patrick across integrated sustainable systems: aquaponics, earth-building/sustainable construction, solar technologies, sustainable forestry, gasifier design, and biofuel/biogas production from waste biomass.
- **Biomass2Methanol (B2M) — her flagship project there, still an active Windward program today.** Her role: "Specialist and Project Lead" — grid-tied solar installation, programming microcontrollers to control production parameters, website design/maintenance, forestry/tree-planting work, and grant writing. She personally authored at least 3 grant proposals found in Drive (Alliance for Justice; a Project Slingshot application; a biofuel grant), and is listed as the program's "Contact person" in a July 2008 grant document. **She also led research on diesel fuel replacement and animal feed from BSFL (black soldier fly larvae).**
- **Presented at a real conference**: "Biomass to Methanol: Combined Heat, Power, Fuel, and Fertilizer," Convergence for Climate Action, 2008.
- **Other projects confirmed** (per an old Wix export and site materials): a 4kW off-grid solar system (including a rotating solar-tracking stand, per 2009 correspondence with a collaborator named Bill) and an aquaponics system.
- **Walt Patrick — Director/Program Manager of Windward since its founding in 1994**, chemistry degree from University of Tennessee at Chattanooga, her direct mentor and co-instructor. In her own words (from a grant-application essay): he "oversaw the installation of eleven septic systems, more than a mile of water lines, some 2,000 amps of electrical service, and the construction of the various buildings" on Windward's 131-acre campus. Still listed on B2M's current team page.
- **Opalyn Patrick — a close working collaborator on gasification/construction engineering**, per 2009–2010 correspondence; still listed on B2M's current team.
- **This is the direct origin point of the ALL Power Labs story** (see `../all-power-labs/README.md` "Origin story"): a Windward group, including Jay, drove to Jim Mason's Berkeley workshop, built one of his early gasifiers, and hauled it back to the Windward farm — where it didn't work. That failure, combined with the 2008 financial crisis costing her her job, is what sent her to Berkeley to ask Jim Mason for work in person.
- **She stayed connected to Windward even after moving on**: a Feb 2009 email shows her still describing herself as a Windward volunteer while living in Portland, and 2009–2010 correspondence (workshops, the solar-stand project) shows an ongoing relationship well past her formal tenure.
- **"The butterfly" — resolved (2026-07-04): this is the AVR Butterfly, an Atmel microcontroller demo board, not an art installation.** Jay used it to monitor and control an off-grid hybrid solar/wind power system. A March 2008 email to Walt Patrick (file `axial flux alternator.doc`) describes the setup directly: *"What we've got now are four 70 watt PV panels wired in series and feeding an Outback MX60 charge controller connected to a pair of deep-draw 12 batterys series up to make 24 volts. That feeds an Outback grid-tie converter. The plan is to use a Butterfly (an Atmel control computer) to monitor the output and control the steam feed..."* A schematic file (`off grid pump.JPG`) shows the AVR Butterfly wired to a relay, LCD display, voltage sensor, and solar panel for pump control. She even had her own compiled firmware file, literally named **`JayPump.hex`**. **This is the true root of a much longer electronics thread**: Windward's own director, Walt Patrick, later independently learned Arduino himself (Oct 2009: "so much easier to use than what came with the Butterfly") — meaning Jay's Windward electronics work sat right at the hobbyist AVR-to-Arduino transition, which continued directly into her APL gasifier-control work and is still ongoing today as a family hobby with her father — see the new `../arduino-projects/README.md`.

## Summary — why this is compelling

Windward is the real headwater of Jay's entire clean-tech/gasification career, not just a line on the resume before ALL Power Labs — a chance meeting with a fellow "hard tech sustainability nerd" at a rained-out Earth Day festival led directly to her first hands-on gasification work, her first exposure to gas-to-liquids chemistry, and the very first mention of Devinder Mahajan's name, years before she'd ever meet him. It's a clean demonstration of how she finds her way into technical fields: not through a formal program or a job posting, but by showing up in person for something niche. It also shows an early pattern of personally owning a program end-to-end — grant writing, technical R&D, microcontroller programming, website maintenance — years before she'd call herself an entrepreneur.
