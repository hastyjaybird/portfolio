## Key facts (2026-07-04 deep dive)

- **Master source**: Jay's own self-sent "art resume" email (Gmail, 2024-08-12): *"The Bay Area Derailleurs, a BMX/freak bike and fire performance troupe (lead organizer, choreography, costume design, stage performances and acting, 2008-2012)."*
- **Date reconciliation, open item**: her 2011 grad-school application draft (Gmail thread "suny app," Jan 2011) says in her own words: *"Since February of last year I have been involved in a women's bike activist group called the Bay Area Derailleurs"* — meaning she personally joined ~Feb 2010, not 2008. The 2008 date in her 2024 resume may reflect the group's founding (see below) rather than her own join date, or may be a retrospective rounding. **Worth confirming with Jay directly** which is accurate for her personal timeline.
- **The group itself**: founded March 2008 by "Agent Chaos" (Eliza Strack, drawing on Sprockettes/Portland bikedance experience) and "Agent Verve" (Hollis Hawthorne, professional dance background). Part of a broader "bikedance" movement with sister troupes nationally/internationally (Sprockettes/Portland, B.C.Clettes and The Brakes/Canada, Velo Vixens, The Spokes/Manchester, Bicycle Belles/Seattle, Gacharinco/Japan).
- **Known member "Agent" names** (from derailleurs.wordpress.com/agents and a 2009 SF Bay Guardian archive article): Agent Chaos, Agent Verve, Agent Flux, Agent Contrary, Agent Joke Star, Agent Agitator, Agent Take the Lane, Agent DoubleOO, Agent Edge, Agent Road Rash (Dawn Andres, joined later). **Jay's own "Agent" name was not found in any source searched — she should confirm it directly**, since it's not in the scraped roster (possibly her records predate/postdate that particular site snapshot, or she used an informal/different name).
- **Confirmed shows/venues**:
  - **Freak Bike Cabaret II**, Friday Feb 11 (2011), presented with Cyclecide, at Cyclecide Swearhouse, 1660 Jerrold Ave, San Francisco. Lineup included Hobogoblins, The Nerv, a kissing booth, "wrestle a Derailleury" date auction, and Derailleurs bikedance. (Poster image: Drive file `freak-bike-cabaret-color.jpg`.)
  - **SF Bike Expo**, Nov 21, 2009 (photo by Richard Masoner/Cyclelicious, Flickr, CC BY-SA 2.0: https://www.flickr.com/photos/bike/4131846450).
  - **The Watershed, Portland**, Feb 19, 2011 (with The Sprockettes).
  - **Oakland show**, May 12, 2011, "Bike Away from Work Party."
  - **Temescal Street Fair, Oakland**, July 10, 2011.
  - Regular practice: Thursdays/Sundays, 7pm, Fell & Masonic (Panhandle Park), SF.
- **Jay's own show-follow-up email** (March 29, 2011, thread "Re: Tell me to send viswo"): *"This is Jay from the Derailleurs thanks for coming to our show! ... that would be super great if you could send [the video] for we totally forgot our video camera."* — confirms she was a recognized, named point-of-contact for the group.
- **Teaching — the best-documented, most substantive part of this story, from her own 2011 grad-school application (in her own words):**
  > "I hold monthly workshops where I teach the women in our team how to weld, use metal lathes, large drill presses and other power tools to do more complex and artistic modifications to bicycles."

  > "I hold monthly workshops where I empower the women in our team by teaching them how to use large industrial equipment such as mig welders, lathes and other power tools to build creative bikes."

  > "Our mission is to educate and energize people to engage in human powered transportation."

  She also listed "work and live in a metal machine shop" as a special qualification on the same application — tying this directly to her Shipyard/APL-era life (see `../all-power-labs/README.md`).
- **Clarified 2026-07-04, per Jay: this is one of two separate instances of the same broader pattern.** She was teaching welding/machining to different groups in different places around the same era — Derailleurs here, and The Shipyard/APL separately (see `../all-power-labs/README.md` and the "Community Building & Third Spaces" section of `docs/full-professional-history-review-2026-07-04.md`). Not the same workshops, not one reclassified into the other.
- **Freak-bike design interest continued for over a decade**: a Sept 21, 2022 Bay Area MakerFarm meeting note lists "Jaybird wants to do freak bike design" under Bike Repair Clinic/Fix-A-Flat programming — showing this wasn't a closed chapter, it fed directly into her later MakerFarm community work.
- **America's Got Talent (AGT) — NOT CONFIRMED, likely misremembered or undocumented.** Jay recalled a possible Derailleurs AGT appearance; a thorough Gmail/Drive/web search found zero corroborating evidence anywhere (no casting correspondence, no video, no press, no mention on the group's own blog/history). **Needs Jay's direct input**: specific season/year and who was involved, so a more targeted search can be attempted — or she may be thinking of a different act entirely.
- **"Dung Beetle" is NOT a Derailleurs performance piece.** Per Jay (2026-07-04), there are two completely unrelated things with this name: (1) a costume character she played at a Take 3 Presents event (Swarm theme camp, 2024 — see `../take-3-presents/README.md`), and (2) the entirely separate AfrikaBurn Dung Beetle Project, a plastic-to-power engineering prototype (2018+, see `../dung-beetle-project/README.md`), which was also the origin story for the hardware company ScarabTech (`../scarab-tech/README.md`). Neither has anything to do with Bay Area Derailleurs.
