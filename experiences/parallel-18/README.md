## Key facts (verified, 2026-07-02)

- **Independently confirmed org background:** Parallel18 was founded in December 2015 by the **Puerto Rico Science, Technology and Research Trust (PR Science Trust)**, modeled on Startup Chile, as a performance-driven international accelerator with a social mission. Its locally-focused pre-accelerator track, **"pre18,"** was added in 2017. As of recent public reporting, P18 has supported 300+ companies, 800+ jobs, and $500M+ in cumulative graduate revenue; pre18 alone has graduated 183+ Puerto Rican companies with a 74% survival rate. Sources: [Parallel18](https://parallel18.com/), [Latin America Reports](https://latinamericareports.com/puerto-ricos-parallel18-startup-accelerator-showcases-its-most-recent-portfolio/8609/).
- **Major finding: Parallel18/PR Science Trust is the literal origin point of ACEPR itself, not a separate, later relationship.** A real chain of Gmail evidence:
  - **Aug 6, 2018:** Marie at Parallel18 personally introduces "Jay Bird, from Box Power" to **Lucy Vazquez and L. Crespo at the PR Science Trust** (Parallel18's parent organization) — explicitly framed around Jay's interest in "energy microgrid regulations with a focus on empowering communities."
  - **Aug 7, 2018:** David Geeter (later a true ACEPR co-founder) circulates a meeting agenda for "Agenda w/Lucy" covering introductions, objectives, "ACEPR as Association and Coalition," network mapping, and existing cooperatives — this is the actual ACEPR founding-strategy meeting, convened within 24 hours of the Parallel18/PR Science Trust introduction.
  - **Aug 13, 2018:** a Corvidae Labs follow-up thank-you email goes out to Ricardo (Espacio Conector), Crespo/Vazquez (PR Science Trust), and Marie (Parallel18) — summarizing the meeting.
  - **Aug 14, 2018:** the actual **"ACEPR DECK"** ("Working External Deck") was created — and it explicitly models ACEPR's business structure as a hybrid of a national utility-cooperative association (like NRECA) **and Parallel18 itself, named directly as the accelerator-model template** ("A start-up accelerator that accepts cohorts and mentors them through development phases... ex: Parallel 18").
  - **Correction found in this same deck:** ACEPR's Spanish name is **"La Aceleradora de Cooperativas Energéticas de Puerto Rico"** ("Accelerator," not "Asociación"/Association; "Energéticas"/energy, not "Eléctricas"/electric) — this differs from the acronym expansion currently on file in `../acepr/README.md` and should be corrected there.
- **Resolved by Jay (2026-07-02): the Nov 2018 P18-agreement question was accelerator business-plan research, not an attempt to join P18 as a cohort.** She was asking Ryan Stobie for a copy of his signed P18 agreement specifically to inform ACEPR's own accelerator business plan design — ACEPR was never exploring becoming a Parallel18 cohort company itself.
- **New finding (Jay, 2026-07-02): ACEPR also modeled itself partly on Puerto Rico's existing water-cooperative movement.** Jay and her co-founders met with the PR Science Trust specifically to ask about the **quarterly water-cooperative meetups held island-wide** (which the Science Trust hosted) — water cooperatives already had a working, proven model on the island for pooling resources to solve a hard shared problem (pumping/filtering water fit for human consumption), and ACEPR explored collaborating with the Science Trust around that model as a second template, alongside Parallel18's accelerator structure.
- A July 2019 email ("Hey I'm at p18 till 12:45," from David Geeter) suggests real in-person presence at Parallel18's physical space, not a purely remote relationship.
- The specific regulatory citations in the Aug 2018 ACEPR deck are now on record: PR Senate bill PS 984 (May 2018) and CEPR Microgrid Development Regulation 9028 (May 2018) — the same two regulatory developments referenced in `../acepr/README.md`'s origin story — plus a newly found detail: **$4 billion in federal funding for energy grid revitalization was announced in September 2018**, cited directly in ACEPR's own founding deck as part of the "why now" case.

## Values/character — why this wasn't "stealing the playbook" (Jay's own account, 2026-07-02)

Jay is explicit that there was no sense of appropriating Parallel18's model for ACEPR's benefit, for a specific reason: **the two organizations weren't competitors or even in the same lane.** Parallel18 was interested in technology startups broadly; ACEPR was strictly a **solar-project accelerator for electric cooperatives**, helping communities whose members were trying to formalize as a legal cooperative entity specifically to be in a better position to access financing. Different domain, different purpose, no overlap to protect.

**She also never took a paycheck from any ACEPR-related project** — she presented herself to the Puerto Rico entrepreneurial and cooperative movement as a volunteer, not a paid consultant. Her read on why Parallel18 was so generous with access, introductions, and eventually a mentorship position: they saw her as being **in solidarity with their movement**, and offered her the mentor role as a genuine **gesture of gratitude** for what she was doing with ACEPR — not a formal, arm's-length appointment.

**On the broader culture (her own words, paraphrased):** Puerto Rico's entrepreneurial and cooperative scene runs on **mutual aid, trust-forward relationships, and overlapping personal interests** — everyone helping everyone else out. Her explicit framing: "it's a small island, you can't get away with anything untrustworthy." That's a genuinely useful piece of context for understanding *why* the Parallel18 relationship worked the way it did — it wasn't transactional networking, it was participation in a small, trust-dense community where reputation and reciprocity do most of the work that formal contracts might do elsewhere.

## Summary — why this is compelling

Parallel18 turns out to be much more than a quiet civic-volunteering line — it's the missing piece connecting how ACEPR actually got built. The dated email trail is unusually clean: a Parallel18 staffer introduces Jay to the PR Science Trust on Aug 6, 2018; a founding-strategy meeting happens the very next day; a thank-you note goes out on the 13th; and by the 14th, Jay and her co-founders have a real pitch deck that explicitly names Parallel18 as the accelerator model ACEPR should copy. That's not "networking helped, eventually" — that's a documented, week-long sprint from first introduction to founding document, with Parallel18 serving simultaneously as the network that made the introduction and the literal template for the organization that came out of it. And on top of that origin role, Jay then spent six years as a standing, recurring judge and mentor for Parallel18's own cohorts — seven "pre18" generations and multiple main-track generations, continuing well past her own move back to California after COVID. It's a rare kind of relationship: an organization that was simultaneously her inspiration, her network, and later the thing she gave back to for years.
