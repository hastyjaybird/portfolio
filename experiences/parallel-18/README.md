## Key facts (verified, 2026-07-02)

- **Origin traces to the same PR post-Maria entrepreneurial network as ACEPR and TeamHouse.** An Aug 13, 2018 email from Corvidae Labs (cc'ing David Geeter — one of ACEPR's true co-founders, see `../acepr/README.md`) thanks a group including **Marie at Parallel18**, Ricardo (Espacio Conector), and contacts at PR Science Trust for a networking conversation — placing the Parallel18 relationship's origin right alongside ACEPR's own founding period.
- **Real, recurring role confirmed via Gmail: Jay served as a judge/mentor for Parallel18's "pre18" pre-accelerator program across at least 7 generations, plus multiple main-track P18 generations, from 2019 through at least October 2024** — a six-year, standing volunteer commitment, not a one-time favor:
  - April 2019: formally introduced as "Pre18 Judge and potential Mentor" (David and Cristina Tamayo at Parallel18); confirmed for pre18 Gen 2.
  - 2020: pre18 Gen 3 judging (twice — spring and summer rounds).
  - 2021: pre18 Gen 4 judging.
  - 2022: pre18 Gen 5 judging.
  - 2023: pre18 Gen 6 judging, plus P18 Gen 11 (main track) judging.
  - 2024: P18 Gen 12 (main track) judging, plus pre18 Gen 7 judging.
- Parallel18 staff Jay corresponded with directly over the years: David, Cristina Tamayo (Business Development Manager), Sylmarie Díaz Cruz (Business Development Associate), Mario (of YouNoodle, the platform P18 used for judging), and later Lissa, Johann, and Leandra.

- No independent public confirmation of Jay's specific judge/mentor status was sought (expected — accelerator judge rosters aren't typically publicized), but the email trail is extensive and unambiguous.

## Summary — why this is compelling

Parallel18 is a good example of something easy to overlook in a resume built around headline ventures: sustained, unglamorous civic volunteering. Jay didn't just judge one cohort as a favor — she kept showing up for Puerto Rico's national accelerator across seven "pre18" generations and multiple main-track generations, spanning six years and surviving her own move back to California after COVID. It's a quiet, consistent form of giving back to the exact entrepreneurial ecosystem that ACEPR and TeamHouse were also part of — evaluating other people's ideas, for free, year after year, long after any single project's clock had run out. That kind of standing commitment says something different from a single flashy engagement: it's evidence of genuine, durable investment in a place and its people, not just a project she happened to work on.
