## DPW Power — volunteer generator-sensor project (one year, per Jay)

- Jay volunteered one year with Burning Man's own **DPW (Department of Public Works) Power team** — not a camp, the event organization's own power infrastructure division — alongside her then-partner **"Monk" (Ricardo Romero Gianoli)**, who onboarded her. They helped put sensors on generators to inform DPW's planning for transitioning the org's own power infrastructure toward renewable energy in future years.
- **Timing, per Jay (2026-07-05):** this happened during the year she and Monk were dating (correspondence places their relationship ~2016–2018).
- A real 2019 "DPW Power Sector Lead" job posting (cross-posted to Jay, explicitly framed as "an excellent position from which to help transition to renewables") confirms DPW Power is a real, distinct Burning Man org department — but no direct email/Drive evidence ties Jay/Ricardo to the specific sensor project. **Very plausibly an entirely in-person/playa arrangement with no email trail** — treated as confirmed per Jay's own account; exact year and technical specifics remain undocumented in writing.

## NiNo Alicea's art (Coquí, Atabey) — CONFIRMED 2026-07-05

- **Oscar Ruiz personally introduced Jay to NiNo Alicea** — not through her broader Puerto Rico professional network generally, specifically through Oscar. Informal, not a paid engagement.
- **"Coquí"** — a real Burning Man Honoraria installation by Puerto Rican artist **NiNo Alicea** (a giant sculpture of the coquí, the singing tree frog, a Puerto Rican cultural symbol). Won Best of Show at the Tahoe Reno International Art Show; toured to Western Lights Festival and the LA County Fair. **Jay helped hook up solar for this piece at Burning Man.**
- **"Atabey"** — also by NiNo Alicea, debuted as a Black Rock City Honoraria installation in 2022 (a fish sculpture representing the Taíno goddess Atabey, a tribute to Puerto Rico's post-Hurricane-Maria environmental challenges, solar-powered). **Jay also helped hook up solar for this piece at Burning Man.**
- **Atabey's Treasure (the SF installation) — this resolves the original "Esplanade, SF" mystery.** NiNo found a second venue for the piece: the **SF Embarcadero Esplanade Art Walk** (Port of San Francisco's Brannan Street Wharf Park, Oct 2025, via nonprofit ReadyToPlayART's "Big Art Loop – Portside" program). **Jay personally helped with installation and troubleshooting power issues when Atabey was installed there.**
- **"Nito" remains unrelated** — a mechanic (Nito@kellystruck.com) repairing Jay's personal vehicle in 2026. No solar or art connection.
