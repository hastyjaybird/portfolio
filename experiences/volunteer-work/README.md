## Entries

### La Finca de Hamberto — Vieques, Puerto Rico (with Oscar Ruiz)
- **When:** Recurring, over the years Jay lived in Puerto Rico (~2018–2020+).
- **Blurb:** Volunteered at La Finca de Hamberto, a regenerative farm and community resilience center on Vieques run by **Oscar Ruiz**, executive director of the nonprofit **Community Through Colors**.
- **Independently confirmed (2026-06-30):** La Finca de Hamberto is a USDA Organic farm, part of the Vieques Agricultural Collective and home to the AVES educational initiative — focused on food sovereignty, ecological resilience, and combating food insecurity on an island that's historically suffered isolation and disaster impacts (Hurricane Maria). The farm also hosts a Cornell-team mobile solar-powered battery that operates independently of Vieques' main grid.
- **Notable: "La Finca de Hamberto, Community Through Colors, and Sail Relief Team form a collaborative network"** (per independent source) — directly connecting this to the Sail Relief work below; this is a sustained volunteer network, not disconnected one-off trips.
- Sources: [lafincadehamberto.com](https://lafincadehamberto.com/), [Civil Eats — Finca Conciencia / Vieques food sovereignty context](https://civileats.com/2019/05/15/finca-conciencia-is-building-food-sovereignty-on-vieques-island/).
