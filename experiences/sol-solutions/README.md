## Why this is its own folder

Split out of the Resilient Markets umbrella per the same client-sweep pattern used for Corvidae, per Jay's request 2026-07-03.

## Key facts (verified, 2026-07-03)

- **SolSolutions LLC**, CEO **Chaz Peling** (also referenced as "Traverse" in some threads), Santa Rosa, CA (880 Piner Rd.) — ran "Sustainable Event Services," a rental business supplying solar generators (SolMan line), solar LED light towers, and a "Mobile Green Power" solar/bio-diesel hybrid trailer for festivals, weddings, winery events, construction sites, and disaster-relief lighting/power.
- **Relationship origin traces back to Jay's APL years (2018):** Chaz first reached out about a demo opportunity/APL bio-digester and offered a 10% referral commission on qualified leads he sent Jay's way — a genuine mutual-referral relationship, not a one-way client engagement.
- **Became a paid Resilient Markets client by 2021:** real Zoom meeting records show a "SolSolutions pre-raise green meeting" (June 2021) and a "Jay/Andy SolSolutions Work Meeting" (June 2021) — Jay was helping SolSolutions prepare for an investment raise.
- **Real work product found:** a full crowdfunding video production plan (shot list, script/messaging outline, target-audience analysis) aimed at raising community investment for SolSolutions via a platform like Raise Green — explicitly targeting wildfire-conscious Sonoma County investors ("made a bunch of money in .com, living in Sonoma, everything is burning, don't have the skill set, want to know I am making an impact"). This is real go-to-market/fundraising creative strategy work, not just advisory conversation.
- **An early warning sign found (April 2021):** a calendar note — "Sol Solutions - stopped website. Is it worth moving forward on a ph 1?" — shows the relationship already had reliability concerns a few months before the deeper 2021 engagement.
- **The relationship ended badly:** a Dec 2022 email from RM partner Mimi Lee references "the correction to the total amount of bad debt owed by Sol Solutions" — confirming SolSolutions left Resilient Markets with unpaid invoices.
- **Company confirmed defunct by 2024:** in an unrelated 2024 warranty-support email (for personal equipment Jay had purchased from them), she states plainly: "I'm aware that the seller is out of business. They were a brick and mortar solar equipment showroom, SolSolutions in Santa Rosa, and many of those have gone out of business in the last couple of years."

## Summary — why this is compelling

Sol Solutions is a useful counterpoint to the tidier success stories elsewhere in this portfolio: a multi-year relationship that started as a genuine mutual-referral partnership from the APL days, matured into real fundraising and creative strategy work for a Resilient Markets client trying to raise a community investment round, and ultimately ended with unpaid debt when the underlying business couldn't survive the broader wave of small solar-equipment retailers going under. It's an honest example of consulting risk — not every client relationship converts into a lasting success, and Jay's own account of the company's failure (grounded in a completely unrelated personal warranty claim years later) shows she tracked the outcome candidly rather than glossing over it.
