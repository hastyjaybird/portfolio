## Key facts (verified, 2026-07-03)

- **SolSolutions LLC**, CEO **Chaz Peling** — Santa Rosa, CA (880 Piner Rd.) — ran "Sustainable Event Services," a rental business supplying solar generators (SolMan line), solar LED light towers, and a "Mobile Green Power" solar/bio-diesel hybrid trailer for festivals, weddings, winery events, construction sites, and disaster-relief lighting/power. **Correction (2026-07-06), per Jay: "Traverse," which appears in some threads, is an employee of Chaz's, not another name for Chaz himself.**
- **Relationship origin (2018), corrected 2026-07-04 per Jay — not connected to APL:** Chaz first reached out about a demo opportunity and offered a 10% referral commission on qualified leads he sent Jay's way — a genuine mutual-referral relationship, not a one-way client engagement.
- **Became a paid Resilient Markets client by 2021:** real Zoom meeting records show a "SolSolutions pre-raise green meeting" (June 2021) and a "Jay/Andy SolSolutions Work Meeting" (June 2021) — Jay was helping SolSolutions prepare for an investment raise.
- **Real work product found:** a full crowdfunding video production plan (shot list, script/messaging outline, target-audience analysis) aimed at raising community investment for SolSolutions via a platform like Raise Green — explicitly targeting wildfire-conscious Sonoma County investors ("made a bunch of money in .com, living in Sonoma, everything is burning, don't have the skill set, want to know I am making an impact"). This is real go-to-market/fundraising creative strategy work, not just advisory conversation.
- **The pivot story (Jay's own account, 2026-07-06; not independently corroborated in Drive/Gmail — a targeted search found no supporting documents, so this is recorded as self-reported):** SolSolutions was originally a brick-and-mortar equipment retailer/installer (solar equipment, batteries) that was **getting crushed by larger solar companies offering in-house financing and online equipment sales** — a classic small-local-retailer-vs.-bigger-player-with-financing squeeze. **Jay personally helped Chaz pivot the company away from that losing retail model and into larger project work.**
- Note (2024, unrelated personal warranty-support email): Jay confirms the retail/showroom side of the business did eventually close — "they were a brick and mortar solar equipment showroom, SolSolutions in Santa Rosa, and many of those have gone out of business in the last couple of years" — consistent with the broader retail-model pressure described above, not treated here as the end of the whole relationship or a failure story.

## Summary — why this is compelling

Sol Solutions is a real business-strategy credential: a multi-year relationship that started as a genuine mutual-referral partnership, where Jay helped a struggling brick-and-mortar solar retailer pivot away from a retail model that larger, better-financed competitors were winning, and toward larger project work — later maturing into real fundraising/creative-strategy work for a Resilient Markets capital raise.
