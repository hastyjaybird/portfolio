## Key facts (2026-07-04 deep dive)

- **What Take 3 Presents is**: a Bay Area event production nonprofit running annual themed events, most prominently **Big Stick Shindig (BSS)**, each with its own yearly theme. Confirmed via a signed 2025 volunteer waiver ("501t3 Volunteer Disclaimer and Waiver Jaybird Hasty... DBA Take 3 Presents") and ongoing T3 newsletters through mid-2026.
- **Master source**: Jay's own self-sent "art resume" email (Gmail, 2024-08-12) — full text:
  > "For Take 3 Presents, they have brought several character shticks related directly to the theme of the event such as *Dr. Oopsie from In-N-Out Medical* (Wayward School, 2022) and *Big Suga of Big Suga Corp* (Fairytale, 2023). They were an integral part in building and running *Big String* theme camp (T3 grant awarded, Fairytale, 2023) and most recently was co-lead on *SWARM* theme camp (T3 grant awarded, Primordial Soup, 2024)."
- Also lists, in the same document, a separate ongoing collective: **"Book Club (coordination, experience design, still and interactive art, 2021-current)"** — not yet independently researched beyond this line; unclear if Book Club is a T3-affiliated group or separate. **Open question for Jay.**

### Characters (theme-specific "shticks")

- **Dr. Oopsie, from "In-N-Out Medical"** — BSS 2022, theme "Wayward School." Only documented in the art-resume line; no script, photos, or further detail found in Gmail/Drive.
- **Big Suga, of "Big Suga Corp"** — BSS 2023, theme "Fairytale." Well-documented: a full character script exists as a Google Doc, **"take 3 - BIG SUGA"** (shared 2023-05-23 by jay@resilientmarkets.com). It's a satirical "Sugar Cartel" conspiracy-theory bit. Verbatim excerpt:
  > "BIG SUGA... The ACT: 1. I am very suspicious that you unknowingly are involved in a great conspiracy. We think you are in grave danger. 2. This is a test to see how much you have been brainwashed... tell them Big Suga sent you. The code is for access to 'The Box' at the misinformation booth... Since you are already in the inside, you can become a spy for Big Sugar. If you find the Himalayan Salt Cult Leader, they need to be taken down immediately."

  Prop list: "purple velvet curtains, black brief case with hand cuffs, humming bird feeder, pill bottle lights, chandeliers, lock box and combination code, cork board decorated with suga and salt chart, candy land." Visual references cited: Aunt Jemima, Queen Bee, Cruella de Vil, Ursula, a gold royal crown, "Sugar Cartel" imagery, "Confectionary Confederate." No costume photos found yet.
- **"Dung Beetle" — a character Jay played as part of the Swarm theme camp** (BSS 2024, "Primordial Soup" theme — see Swarm below), fitting the insect-carnival concept. **Important: this is a completely separate thing from "The Dung Beetle Project"** (AfrikaBurn, Alliance Earth/Jeff Barbee's plastic-to-power gasification system) — same name, zero connection. See `../dung-beetle-project/README.md` for that entirely different project, and do not conflate the two in any materials.

### Theme camps

- **Big String** (BSS 2023, "Fairytale" theme, T3 grant awarded) — Jay's own words: "were an integral part in building and running." Payment records ("Expenses for Big String BSS22/BSS23") list "Jaybird" as crew/purchaser. Build ran spring 2023 (event June 8-12, 2023). Drive docs: "BigString Dash Board!" (fabric/shade-structure inventory — large velvet/canvas panels, shade structure with dormers), budget doc (~$1,865 spent: $300 T3 grant + $812 GoFundMe).
- **Swarm** (BSS 2024, "Primordial Soup" theme, T3 grant awarded, Jay co-lead) — an insect-carnival concept camp ("Cockroach Prepper Bunker, Hive Mind recruitment, Pupation Station, Spider Yoga"). Led by Anja Ulfeldt (UC Santa Cruz/Stanford lecturer); ~15-person crew including Christopher Hann-Soden, Susan Hepp, Michael Morin, Miles Pekala, Heidi Petty, Tiffany Harker. Build days ran ~April-June 2024 (Sunday "crafternoon" sessions at Alameda MakerFarm). Drive docs: brainstorm doc, T3 grant proposal ("Draft 2/3"), budget ($1,500-2,000), receipts, activity station designs, an audio file "swarm 1.mp3." T3 staff (Kaizo Naka) followed up in Feb 2025 connecting a prospective artist "interested in your build last year" — confirming Swarm's reputation within T3.
- **Church of Launder Day Saints** (BSS 2026 — correct spelling "Launder Day," not "Launderday") — Jay's most recent and most senior role: **listed as "Jaybird (Build Lead)"** in the official Illustrated Proposal team bios. A punning concept ("Latter Day Saints" + laundry/cleanliness rituals) — "ritual cleansing of the body and spirit," juxtaposing a laundry-ritual "Temple" against an indulgence-purging "House of Filth." Her bio in that proposal document (worth quoting, since it's a real self-authored summary used for this grant): *"Jaybird has participated with several art collectives... including Flaming Lotus Girls (combustion design, controls, welding, fabrication, fire safety and fire marshal approvals), Book Club..., and The Bay Area Derailleurs... As for Take 3, they have volunteered, brought unsponsored art, and collaborated on sponsored art, including: Dr. Oopsie..., Big Suga..., The Big String (BSS '23) and stage build and props (RS '22)."* Team: lead Christopher Hann-Soden; Susan Hepp (Comms); Juno Woods (Experience Design); Miles Pekala (Mechanical/Structural); Michael Morin (Electronics); Bryan Winters (Strike Lead); Tiffany Harker (Stage Manager); Beth Winters, Cole Amick, Heidi Petty, Samstrom (Artists). Proposal deadline March 1, 2026; build/event window ~June 8-16, 2026. Drive assets: a 3D render (`Church_of_Launderday_Saints_render.png`), a 31MB "Illustrated Proposal" slide deck, content-warning slides, rideshare/meal planning spreadsheet, credits spreadsheet.
- **Also newly found via the Church of Launder Day Saints bio**: a **"Room Service '22" (RS '22) stage build and props** credit — not otherwise documented, worth folding in.
