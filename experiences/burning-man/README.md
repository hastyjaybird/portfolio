## Why this is its own folder

Split out 2026-07-05, per Jay's request — DPW Power, IDEATE, the NiNo/Coquí/Atabey art connection, Fish Bug, La Attrata, Burners Without Borders (BWB), and LAGI Fly Ranch aren't really related to each other except that they're all part of the same broader Burning Man involvement. This umbrella collects them; each has (or shares) its own dedicated folder for full detail.

## Sub-projects

- **[IDEATE](../ideate/README.md)** — a Burning Man theme camp (founded ~2011). Jay's power/solar team role (member 2015 → coordinator 2016 → **Lead 2017**), and — separately — she was a key driver (alongside Sandra Kwak) of IDEATE's camp-composting/prison-disposal pipeline in 2015, which grew into Burning Man's first playa-wide multi-camp compost program.
- **[DPW Power / Burners Without Borders / NiNo art connection — see `../burning-man-solar/README.md`]** for full detail on:
  - **DPW Power** — Jay volunteered one year with Burning Man's own Department of Public Works Power team (the event organization's own power infrastructure, not a camp), alongside her then-partner "Monk" (Ricardo Romero Gianoli), helping put sensors on generators to inform DPW's renewable-transition planning.
  - **Burners Without Borders (BWB)** — a real, 8-year (2018–present) relationship, not just a recent one: Jay joined BWB's Black Rock City camp in 2018, connected into BWB's broader civic/disaster-relief lineage (Black Rock Labs infrastructure-sustainability work, 2019), and has functioned for years as a networked technical/solar resource person feeding BWB-descended humanitarian projects — including Sail Relief Team's Puerto Rico work (see `../volunteer-work/README.md`) and a 2026 AfrikaBurn solar connection.
  - **NiNo Alicea's art (Coquí, Atabey)** — still unconfirmed whether Jay worked directly on these Puerto Rico-themed Burning Man installations; flagged as open.
- **[Fish Bug — see `../flaming-lotus-girls/README.md`]** — a real 2009 Burning Man sculpture ("Chimera Sententia," built by Rebecca Anders and Jess Hobbs at The Shipyard). Jay's connection is general Shipyard-community overlap during the same era, not a confirmed hands-on build role — flagged as open in that folder.
- **[La Attrata — see `../le-attrata-therm/README.md`]** — the 40-foot fire-moth sculpture (Therm collective, Burning Man 2016) where Jay did welding, combustion-system design, and electronics/LED control work.
- **[LAGI 2020 Fly Ranch Design Challenge — see `../land-art-generator-initiative/README.md`]** — a renewable-energy design competition run in direct partnership with the Burning Man Project, sited at Fly Ranch (Burning Man's own permanent land). Jay served as Technical Advisor/Shortlisting Committee juror, Nov 2020–March 2021 — **newly confirmed connection (2026-07-05): she was recruited for this specifically because of the BWB camp community she'd been part of since 2018** ("We met at BWB camp back at Burning Man 2018 where we were just announcing the LAGI 2020 Fly Ranch design challenge").

## Other Burning Man-adjacent context (not part of this umbrella, but worth knowing about)

- **AfrikaBurn** (South Africa's "Burning Man," a distinct sister event) is where Scarab Tech and The Dung Beetle Project both originated — see `../scarab-tech/README.md` and `../dung-beetle-project/README.md`. Kept separate since it's a different event, not Black Rock City.
- **Take 3 Presents / Big Stick Shindig** (`../take-3-presents/README.md`) is a separate Bay Area event production org, not Burning Man itself, though it shares a lot of cultural/community DNA.
- A minor, tangential mention: an informal fire-safety advisory Jay gave friends on a separate Burning Man fire-art installation ("Garden of Firely Delights," reviewed by Burning Man's own FAST safety team) — see `../music-creative/README.md`; not her own project.
- **All Power Labs** (`../all-power-labs/README.md`) — Jay was invited onto a 2019 "Black Rock Labs" infrastructure-sustainability shortlist committee (the same Black Rock Labs effort referenced under BWB above).
- **10Power** (`../10power/README.md`) — Jay and Sandra Kwak's friendship traces back to a 2016 Burning Man trip (a borrowed-solar-panels anecdote), predating their business relationship.
- **Community houses** (`../community-houses/README.md`) — sculptor Michael Christian (landlord/APL shareholder) is a 20-time Burning Man arts-grant recipient; "Flava Packet" served as a Burning Man-prep home base in 2016.

## Why this is compelling

Ten-plus years of real, escalating involvement in one community: from camp-level power/solar leadership and a hands-on citywide sustainability win (the composting program), to volunteering directly with the event organization's own infrastructure team, to being tapped by the Burning Man Project itself for a technical advisory role on its own land — and, running underneath all of it, a genuine multi-year role as a technical/solar connector feeding real humanitarian projects (Puerto Rico disaster recovery, and now Africa) that trace their lineage back to Burners Without Borders. It's a rare case of volunteer community involvement compounding into a recognized technical-advisory relationship with the organization itself, the same pattern that shows up in Jay's professional career (ACEPR, MaxETA) — just playing out in a different world.
