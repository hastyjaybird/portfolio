## Key facts (verified)

- PhD advisor: Devinder Mahajan (also PI/TL on NanoSulf and CPWater Tech — see those folders).
- Adjunct teaching: Senior Design, Chemical Engineering.
- **Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL) collaboration — confirmed (2026-06-30):**
  - Independently confirmed online: Mahajan held a **Joint Scientific Staff Appointment in the Sustainable Energy Technologies Department at Brookhaven National Laboratory, Sept 2002 – Sept 2015** — covering Jay's entire PhD (2010–2015). His lab was formally a joint Stony Brook/BNL research group the whole time. Source: [SBU News — Jefferson Science Fellowship](https://news.stonybrook.edu/news/general/jeffersonsciencefellow).
  - **Confirmed directly from Jay's own official NSF paperwork:** her NSF SBIR "Collaborators and Other Affiliations" disclosure form lists, verbatim: *"Mahajan, Devinder — Stony Brook, Brookhaven National Labs — Collaborator, Graduate PhD Advisor."*
  - **Confirmed via Gmail:** Jay was directly cc'd on multi-year email threads with BNL scientist **vbhatt@bnl.gov**, as part of the US-China EcoPartnership/Sino-US Energy Forum collaboration (see below) — as far back as an October 2014 thread that also cc'd Mahajan's actual BNL email address (dmahajan@bnl.gov) directly.
  - **Do not conflate:** the NanoSulf catalyst pilot site was the "Town of Brookhaven Landfill" on Long Island — a municipal landfill named after the Town of Brookhaven, unrelated to Brookhaven National Laboratory (which happens to sit within that same town). Keep these two "Brookhaven"s separate in any materials.

  - Stony Brook's EcoPartnership was officially signed with **Tongji University**, focused on landfill biogas control and reuse, as part of the U.S. State Department's official **U.S.-China EcoPartnerships** program. The signing ceremony was held before **U.S. Deputy Secretary of State William Burns**; Mahajan represented Stony Brook. Source: [SBU News — Ecopartnership with Tongji University](https://news.stonybrook.edu/newsroom/press-release/general/ecopartnershipwithtongjiuniversity/).
  - **Changsha, China forum, Dec 3–5, 2017** — "Energy and Environment Forum: Monetizing Fugitive Gases through Harvesting and Utilization," co-organized by Stony Brook and Tongji.   - A second forum in **Beijing, September 2019** under the same ongoing partnership was already logged separately (from the Wix scrape).
