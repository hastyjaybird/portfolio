## Key facts (verified)

- Run by **Eli Whipple** (eli@newpowerindustry.com), an inventor/industrial engineer/fabricator (renewable fuels & energy background, first patent filed 2014) pursuing a distributed-manufacturing business concept: **"Distributing Manufacturing — enabling agency and dignity in how we earn a living,"** explicitly framed as **"Transitioning from Maximum Extraction to Maximum Value Densification."**
- **Ventures within the studio that Jay personally worked on (per Jay, 2026-07-06 — other NPI ventures found publicly, e.g. Oakland Craft Distillers, E-Lite Studio, Regenovation Ltd., and a biochar/forestry concept, are excluded here since she didn't work on them):**
  - **Ethanol Revolution** — developing the "Freedom Still 3500," described as the world's first fully automated ethanol distillery for small-scale producers; Jay's own 2022–2023 newsletter record shows it in active prototype/product development (an "automation box" prototype, targeting a functional pilot unit by Feb 2023) and running a WeFunder equity-crowdfunding round ($50K goal, $100 minimum buy-in).
  - **BetterStove** — a clean biomass cookstove program Jay also worked on directly; per the 2023 newsletter, batches were shipped to Southern California and then to Kenya for field trials (arrived Nov 2022), with durability/stress testing to follow.
  - **Eco-Industrial Village Network (EIVN)** — a distributed-manufacturing-community initiative (see below for Jay's specific contribution).
- **What Jay specifically did:**
  - **2020** — early advisory role during the "Ethanol Revolution" phase of the studio: reviewed pitch materials, was asked for a bio/headshot for the pitch deck being sent to investors, and discussed a formal Advisor Agreement — indicating she was a named/considered advisor on the pitch deck itself.
  - **Sept 2021** — advisory calls specifically on the New Power Industry business plan.
  - **March 2022** — direct, documented contribution to a branding/positioning strategy session for the studio's **Eco-Industrial Village Network (EIVN)** initiative, alongside Eli and several other collaborators. Her specific contribution (from the session notes): consumer-brand strategy — "Consumers identify with a brand strongly if the brand's attributes are relatable... The quality of your products, the nature of support you provide, your organizational culture, and your values will shape your brand's identity" — which fed into the group's conclusion that the network's brand archetype should be "Magician" (secondarily Creator/Caregiver).
  - **BetterStove** — **technical and design advisory work during the stove's design and testing phase** (per Jay, 2026-07-06), consistent with the field-trial/durability-testing timeline in the 2023 newsletter (batches to Southern California, then Kenya, Nov 2022).
  - Relationship confirmed continuous 2020–2023 via recurring newsletters, Zoom meetings ("NPI updates with RM"), and correspondence.

## Summary — why this is compelling

New Power Industry is a good example of a client relationship surviving Jay's own corporate transition — Eli Whipple came in as a Corvidae client and moved seamlessly into Resilient Markets when the practice restructured, continuing a genuine multi-year advisory relationship spanning three distinct efforts inside the studio: early pitch-deck/advisor positioning and ongoing business-plan review for Ethanol Revolution, hands-on brand strategy for the Eco-Industrial Village Network, and direct technical/design advisory on the BetterStove clean-cookstove program during its design and testing phase. It's a good example of Jay's advisory range across a single client — financial/business-plan review, consumer-brand strategy, and hands-on hardware/design advisory all in one relationship.
