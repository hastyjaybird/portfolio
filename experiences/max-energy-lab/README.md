## Key facts (verified, 2026-07-01)

- Client: **Max Freidzon**, an independent solo inventor running "Max Energy Lab" — project/technology internally called "Sol," technology itself branded **"Apollo."**
- **Real technical description found** (from "Sol.pdf," Max's own NSF project-description document, Oct 12, 2019): Apollo is a photonic-nanostructure technology using a **"focon" (focusing cone)** to collect and spectrally sort incoming sunlight into an optimized lateral waveguide, where **Longitudinal Incidence Transducers (LIT)** — as opposed to conventional Transverse Incidence Transducers (TIT) — convert light to electricity with a much longer photon-junction interaction path. Claimed target: 22–32% efficiency vs. 15–22% for existing mass-produced solar cells. The same focon geometry was also pitched for non-solar applications: reversible operation for thin, inexpensive 3D displays, and a passive/hybrid mode acting like an e-ink-style trans-reflective pixel.
- **Real NSF SBIR invitation found and confirmed** ("Fwd: Project Pitch 00017520," May 7, 2020, from NSF SBIR/STTR Program Director Steve Konsek): Max was invited to submit a full Phase I proposal (SBIR: Photonics topic area), valid through May 7, 2021. **Important nuance not previously captured: this invitation came with pointed, skeptical reviewer feedback** — Konsek explicitly told Max the project "still looks to be more of a basic research proposal," that the commercialization path and business model weren't clear, that the technical area has been "picked over," and that Max hadn't yet addressed earlier requests for a clearer differentiation from prior attempts. This is a real, conditional invitation with serious open questions, not an endorsement — **do not upgrade this to "won an SBIR award" or even "on track for an award" in any material.**
- **Team context:** Max's sister, **Aleksandra Freidzon**, was brought in as the team's photonic-materials specialist (has a ResearchGate profile and published papers in the space). A partner named **"Robert" in Puerto Rico** also joined the project as of Oct 2019, per Max's own message — unconfirmed who Robert is or whether this connects to Jay's own Puerto Rico network.
- **No contract, NDA, invoice, or payment record found anywhere (checked Gmail and Drive directly, 2026-07-01) — confirmed by Jay's own question flagging this.** The relationship stayed at informal technical/business advisory from Oct 2019. Jay did float a specific paid arrangement on May 26, 2020 ("Would you like to hire me on an hourly basis to support grant writing at a fair price that works for both of us? Your project is worth it!"), but there's no record of Max accepting, no signed agreement, and no invoice or payment of any kind — unlike every other engagement in the Corvidae client set (Celeres, Genmoji, Charm Industrial, C-Change Labs, etc. all have signed contracts/invoices on file). **This should be characterized as unpaid informal advisory, not a completed paid consulting engagement**, unless Jay recalls otherwise.
- No public web presence found for Max Freidzon or "Max Energy Lab" beyond Aleksandra's academic profile — expected for an unfunded solo inventor; the Drive files (project description, NSF invitation letter) are the only available primary sources.

## Why Jay engaged anyway, unpaid (her own account, 2026-07-01)

Jay did a bit of work with Max because she found the underlying physics **technically interesting** — a genuine curiosity-driven engagement, not a business decision. Her own retrospective assessment is clear-eyed and unsentimental: Max couldn't pay her, and he didn't have a solid team together (beyond his sister Aleksandra as the photonics specialist) — and in her judgment, there was nothing she was actually going to be able to do to get him "over the hump." This is a good, honest example of Jay recognizing the limits of what advisory time alone can fix when the underlying team/funding situation isn't there — she didn't chase the engagement past the point where her involvement could plausibly change the outcome.
