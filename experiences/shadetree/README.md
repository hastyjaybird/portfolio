## Key facts (2026-07-04 deep dive) — publicly documented, real project

- **Location/history**: 5th Avenue Point, Oakland — the last stretch of undeveloped private waterfront on the estuary connecting Lake Merritt to the Bay. Robert A. Schultz bought the half-acre property in 1979, renting space to artists/tradespeople for decades, converting industrial space into live/work housing. Property changed hands in 2008 (sold to Reed and Matt Westphal).
- **The crisis (Jay lived through this in real time)**: September 2016 — new owners gave the ~18-30 residents notice they'd need to buy the property or it would likely be sold to a developer for one of Oakland's largest condo projects since WWII.
- **The cooperative response**: residents formed the nonprofit **SHADE (Shadetree Historical Artisan Development Engine)**, led by resident/housing advocate **Donna Smithey**. They raised **$2.5-2.8 million** from **21-22 individual lender-supporters** plus one institutional lender (Val Chris Investments, $1.25M) in about 11 months. February 2017: purchase agreement signed. July 2017: sale completed — the SHADE nonprofit now owns the building outright, with rents averaging ~$900/unit, well below market.
- **Recognition**: an Oakland Heritage Alliance stewardship award; KQED and NPR features; cited by Oakland's Arts Policy Director Kelley Kahn as *"an important model for other communities of artists trying to find the wherewithal to buy their building in Oakland."* Later partnered with the Bay Area Community Land Trust (BACLT) and received a City of Oakland renovation loan (2021). Donna Smithey went on to found the Cultural Impact Fund to replicate the model elsewhere.
- Currently ~30 residents across three generations — artists, carpenters, musicians, social workers.
- Sources: [KQED — "With Luxury Development on All Sides, Oakland Artists Buy the Right to Stay Put"](https://www.kqed.org/arts/13838421/with-luxury-development-on-all-sides-oakland-artists-buy-the-right-to-stay-put), [Bay Area Community Land Trust — Shadetree](https://www.bayareaclt.org/our-houses/shadetree-artists-cooperatve), [KQED — "Art Studios Saved as Oakland Community Land Trust Acquires First Live-Work Building"](https://www.kqed.org/arts/13839406/art-studios-saved-as-oakland-community-land-trust-acquires-first-live-work-building), [Shadetree Artisans](https://www.shadetreeartisans.org/), [Berkeleyside — "Oakland artists buy the right to stay put"](https://www.berkeleyside.org/2018/08/10/oakland-artists-buy-the-right-to-stay-put-as-luxury-homes-rise-up-around-them).
