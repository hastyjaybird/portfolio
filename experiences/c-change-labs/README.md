## Key facts (verified, 2026-07-01)

- **Real signed contract and NDA found** ("Contract for C-Change Labs," Nov 16, 2017; "TwoWayNDA-CChange-Hasty," signed same day) — subject: "Conduct 'first-pass' feasibility study on a gasifier/ammonia project." Fixed lump-sum design fee of $450 (process flow diagram, mass-flow calculator, findings report), additional hours billed at $45/hr. Final invoice: $540 total (12 hours) for work Nov 7–Dec 14, 2017; paid via PayPal Feb 12-13, 2018 with a note from Phil Northcott: "Great working with you! Looking forward to future opportunities."
- **Actual technical scope (corrected from the earlier placeholder description):** this was **not** carbon-accounting software consulting — it was a hands-on process-engineering feasibility study evaluating whether **renewable ammonia** could be produced from a **biomass gasifier's syngas**. Jay built a mass/energy-flow calculator and process flow diagram, and researched commercially available small-scale ammonia synthesis technology (Thyssenkrupp's small-scale ammonia plants, Casale's licensed technology, Proton Ventures' electrolysis-based ammonia systems).
- **Real technical finding/negative result:** working through the numbers with Phil, Jay determined that the hydrogen available from gasifier syngas **wasn't in the right order of magnitude** to support ammonia synthesis at the scale needed — the required hydrogen purity from one vendor's system (Proton Ventures) was ~99.9995%, essentially impossible to hit from raw syngas. The joint conclusion: hydrogen would need to come from **water electrolysis** instead, not from biomass gasification. This is a clean, concrete example of a feasibility study reaching an honest negative/redirect finding rather than confirming what the client hoped — the same "verify, don't rubber-stamp" instinct seen elsewhere in Jay's due-diligence work (see `../celeres/README.md`).
- Independently confirmed online: **Phil Northcott**, founder/CEO of C-Change Labs, based in Port Moody, BC — 22 years of commercial R&D across telecom, solid-state drives, and climate tech. The company's own flagship product is the **BIM Carbon Action Tool (BIMCAT)**, a Revit plugin for supply-chain GHG accountability — this is C-Change Labs' primary business, but it was **not** the subject of Jay's specific engagement, which was a side technical feasibility question about ammonia production. Source: [c-change-labs.com/about-us](https://www.c-change-labs.com/about-us).
- **Timing/context:** this was one of Jay's very first Corvidae engagements — signed less than two months after Corvidae's founding, and running concurrently with her tail-end APL retainer (per Jay's own "Project Pipeline and Payments" tracking spreadsheet, which also shows early Corvidae clients like Devinder Mahajan/Stony Brook Powerbridge work and a "Superman"/Dillon Lanius hydrovac job from the same period) — good evidence Corvidae was hitting the ground running with real, if small, paying engagements from day one.

## Summary — why this is compelling

C-Change Labs is a small engagement by dollar value ($540 total), but it's a clean example of Jay doing honest technical work rather than telling a client what they wanted to hear: asked to assess whether a biomass gasifier could feed a renewable-ammonia process, she and Phil Northcott worked the actual mass/energy balance and hydrogen-purity numbers together and concluded the gasifier route didn't pencil out — the hydrogen would have to come from electrolysis instead. That's a real, falsifiable technical claim getting tested and revised in real time, on a good-faith handshake basis with a repeat-minded client (Phil's own parting note was "looking forward to future opportunities").
