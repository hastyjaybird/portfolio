## Values/character — why TeamHouse mattered (Jay's own account, 2026-07-01)

Jay's own framing is notably precise: TeamHouse "was almost like a networking partner for just about every connection I had in Puerto Rico" — she'd go so far as to formally describe **Team House as a networking partner to Corvidae, LLC itself**, not just a place she happened to live. Because residency was curated and application-only, everyone in the house was already vetted for fit with the working community Gus had built — so simply living there, as a paying (if subsidized) tenant, put her in constant proximity to exactly the kind of people and projects she needed for her Corvidae work. Meeting both Gus and Adam Krim at the same time, through the same networked circle, is a clean illustration of how tightly interconnected her whole Puerto Rico chapter actually was — TeamHouse, ACEPR, and Celeres all trace back to the same small, dense network rather than being separate, disconnected relationships.

## Summary — why this is compelling

TeamHouse is a good illustration of how Jay built out her Puerto Rico network deliberately rather than accidentally: rather than networking from the outside, she embedded herself as a paying tenant inside a curated, application-only community specifically built to cluster compatible founders and projects together. That single housing decision ended up functioning as a *de facto* business-development channel for Corvidae — the same house produced her ACEPR co-founder (Gus) and a Celeres Capital contact (Adam Krim) she'd go on to do paid due-diligence work with over a year later. It's a strong, concrete example of how she thinks about place and proximity as a growth lever, not just a place to sleep.
