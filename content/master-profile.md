### 3. Puerto Rico energy transition (2017–2020+)

- **Situation:** Post-Hurricane Maria; new energy regulation; commercial portfolio resilience needs.
- **Task:** Microgrid development and policy navigation for PR clients — two distinct projects, not to be conflated.
- **Action (ACEPR):** Post-Maria, Puerto Rico endured the longest and most deadly power outage in U.S. history; funding for new ventures was extremely difficult to raise in that environment. PREB (Puerto Rico Energy Bureau) had just issued regulation allowing electric utility cooperatives to form and operate for the first time — when the opportunity broke open island-wide, co-founded ACEPR to organize the emerging industry (cooperatives + solar/battery installers) and capture the momentum. Projects spanned **a large hydroelectric dam**, a **150-building mixed residential/commercial portfolio**, and **800 distributed homes in a mountainous community**. PREB was overwhelmed by the volume of entities needing certification/regulation; worked directly with PREB to streamline their certification process and hold public comment.
- **Action (IOGS):** Founded/led IOGS (Industrial Off-Grid Systems) — an ISO/virtual-IPP model for a **51-building, ~5,000,000 sq ft industrial park** portfolio (Guardiola Real Estate, backed by Remedy Ventures), representing 74% of all industrial real estate on the island. Contemplated **25.2 MW / 75.6 MWh** battery storage capacity sized for 3-day critical-load autonomy. Managed RFP process, EPC/architect/structural-engineer coordination, and PREPA utility account transitions for tenants. Personally built the project timeline and financial model, including the Qualified Opportunity Zone (QOZ) tax compliance structure that let the renewable energy infrastructure serve as a tax write-off for the partners.
- **Also:** Lived in PR ~2 years.
- **Result (ACEPR — cohort projects, names withheld):**

### 6. NanoSulf — customer discovery and an honest limitation (2015–2018)

- **Situation:** Devinder Mahajan's Stony Brook research group (Institute of Gas Innovation and Technology, I-GIT, housed within the Advanced Energy Research and Technology Center/AERTC) had a promising nano-catalyst (oxidative sulfur removal + polishing bed with a bed-life sensor) for scrubbing H2S out of landfill/biogas streams down to <2ppm — a real technical problem, since untreated H2S corrodes equipment, triggers regulatory fines (~$37K/day), and blocks landfills from monetizing gas-to-energy projects.
- **Task:** As Entrepreneurial Lead (pre-seed, pre-launch), run customer discovery to validate market fit and pricing while the technical team (Mahajan as PI/TL, David Tonjes as Co-TL, researchers Lori Clark and Taejin Kim) developed the catalyst itself.
- **Action:** Personally conducted **100+ customer interviews** across landfill gas operators (e.g., Brookhaven Landfill, Waste Management/Altamont), EPCs (Unitel Technologies, SCS Engineers), and consultants; built market segmentation, business plan and pricing model and go-to-market strategy (landfill ~$90M/yr, wastewater/AD ~$1.3B, oil & gas ~$300B) (PowerBridge NY accelerator, 3rd Cohort).
- **Result:** The market validation work was sound — clear segments, real pricing, a signed test-cell customer at Brookhaven Landfill — but the venture stalled in the pre-seed gap between bench validation and a funded pilot. I moved on to other ventures (BoxPower, XPrize, ACEPR).

- **Action:** Nano-catalyst synthesis (via sonication), characterization (TEM/SEM/IR/Raman), and performance testing for syngas-to-mixed-alcohol conversion; publications and conference presentations including COP21. In parallel, sourced, price-negotiated, installed, and commissioned the lab's core instrumentation directly with vendors (PerkinElmer for GC/GC-MS, Gow-Mac for detector replacement, Omega Engineering for flow control, Parr/Equilibar for reactor back-pressure regulation) — confirmed via 2013 vendor correspondence, PO numbers, and her own equipment-inventory presentations to her advisor.
