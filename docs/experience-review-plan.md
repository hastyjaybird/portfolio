## Per-experience loop (repeat for every experience folder)

For each experience, in order:

1. **Research completeness assessment.** Check what's already in the folder (`README.md`, `articles/`, `web-exports/`, `pdfs/`, `images/`, `videos/`, `urls.md`) against what's findable in Jay's Gmail/Drive and online (YouTube videos, URLs, papers, PDFs, logos, images, video). Every experience needs **at least a logo and one good photo/image** suitable for the portfolio site (non-commercial use — don't worry about copyright clearance). Note what's missing.
2. **Report back — updated 2026-07-01, per Jay's instruction.** After collecting and analyzing resources for this experience, give Jay three things before moving to step 3: (a) a narrative summary/story of the experience as currently understood — not just a bullet list of facts, but the shape of the story so far — (b) an itemized list of every resource now in hand for this experience (images, videos, articles, PDFs, with filenames/paths), and (c) **update `docs/missing-resources.md` with a dedicated entry for this experience listing every media/URL/resource gap found that Jay herself would need to go track down** (things no amount of Gmail/Drive/web searching can resolve — e.g., a specific photo that may only exist on her phone, a logo that was never digitized, confirmation only she can give). This is a standing human-facing TODO list, not just an AI research log. Flag explicitly if research is incomplete.
3. **Ask questions to fill out the full picture.** Focus on: why this experience mattered *at the time*, what it reveals about Jay's values, and what it shows about her character — not just facts/dates/numbers.
3a. **Add both a structured record AND a narrative wrap-up — clarified 2026-07-01, per Jay's instruction.** Keep all existing bullet-point facts/quotes exactly as they are (needed as structured source material for other products) — **never replace or condense them.** In addition, append a **"Summary — why this is compelling" section written in flowing prose**, in the same storytelling/"sell" voice used in conversational responses (e.g., "this gives Jay real, direct exposure to X — a notable credential for Y angle"). This prose summary is the reusable long-form-bio material; the bullets remain the structured backing detail. Do both, don't choose one.
4. **Check for done, then evaluate.** Ask if Jay is done adding to this experience. Once she says yes, evaluate it as a resume/portfolio candidate — specifically:
   - How strong a candidate is this for the resume/portfolio?
   - Where could it be expanded to better align with what Anthropic wants for these specific roles and Anthropic's values?
   - Explicitly flag any missed opportunity to communicate values alignment.
5. **Fix open items found during this pass.** Any discrepancy, wrong date, `[VERIFY]`/`[CONFIRM]` flag, or inconsistency discovered while reviewing this experience gets resolved immediately if resolvable (check Gmail/Drive/web, ask Jay if needed) and propagated to every file that repeats the fact (`master-profile.md`, `experience-database.md`, resume, cover letter, interview session files, other experience READMEs that cross-reference it) — don't leave it for a separate cleanup pass. Log what was found and fixed in the "Open items log" section below.
6. **Administrative notes.** Ask if Jay wants to add any admin/process notes to the file before moving to the next experience.

Then move to the next experience in the list below.
