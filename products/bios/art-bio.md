Julia "Jay" Hasty — known in Bay Area maker and fire-art circles as **"Jaybird"** — is a PhD materials scientist whose parallel creative practice spans fifteen-plus years of Bay Area fire art, performance, theme-camp building, and DIY maker culture. Her work sits at the intersection of rigorous technical craft (welding, combustion systems, electronics/LED control) and public-facing creative expression — she brings the same hands-on engineering discipline from her professional life directly into large-scale fire sculpture, immersive theme camps, and character performance.

**Fire art & large-scale sculpture:**

- **Flaming Lotus Girls** (2009–2011) — welding, fabrication, and fire safety with one of the Bay Area's most established fire-art collectives.
- **Le Attrata** (Therm collective, Burning Man 2016) — welding, combustion-system design, and electronics/LED control on a 40-foot kinetic fire sculpture of three giant steel moths, featuring 2,000+ programmable LEDs. Later exhibited at Chatsworth House, UK. *(See `experiences/le-attrata-therm/README.md` for full detail; note there is a live, sensitive credit dispute over this piece as of 2024 that should be handled carefully in any public materials.)*
- **The Dung Beetle Project** (AfrikaBurn, South Africa, 2018–present) — paid technical/business consulting on a large-scale mutant-vehicle sculpture that converts plastic waste to fuel via gasification, applying her clean-tech engineering background directly to an art installation. Ongoing collective membership through 2026.

**Performance & character work:**

- **The Bay Area Derailleurs / Freak Bike Cabaret** (2008–2012) — lead organizer, choreographer, costume designer, and performer in a BMX/freak-bike and fire performance troupe. Also personally ran **monthly workshops teaching welding, metal-lathe operation, and power-tool use**, explicitly framed as empowerment for the women in the group, to build custom "freak bikes."
- **Take 3 Presents** (2022–present) — brought original character performances tied to annual event themes: **Dr. Oopsie, from "In-N-Out Medical"** (2022, "Wayward School") and **Big Suga, of "Big Suga Corp"** (2023, "Fairytale," a satirical Sugar Cartel conspiracy-theory bit with a full staged script).

**Theme camp building:**

- **Big String** (Take 3 Presents, Big Stick Shindig 2023, "Fairytale" theme, T3 grant awarded) — core build/run crew.
- **Swarm** (Big Stick Shindig 2024, "Primordial Soup" theme, T3 grant awarded) — co-lead of an insect-carnival immersive camp.
- **Church of Launder Day Saints** (Big Stick Shindig 2026) — **Build Lead** on a fully sponsored art installation, her most senior role to date in this world.

**DIY/maker instruction & public engagement:**

- **"Helmahawks"** (Instructables, Jan 2012) — a DIY youth bike-safety project: LED-lit mohawk sculptures mounted on bike helmets, built with Monkeyelectric and Bay Area Derailleurs, designed to make safety gear something kids actually want to wear.
- A broader **"make your own ___" DIY-empowerment article series** — including instructions for bike-tube leather goods (trench coats, bags, belts) repurposing discarded inner tubes.
- **Monkeyelectric** (2009–2010) — quality control, packaging, and prototyping for the first persistence-of-vision LED bike-wheel product to market (later sold nationally via REI); founder Dan Goldwater personally taught her circuit-board design and LED programming during this period.
- **Music**: an active, sustained personal practice — ukulele, guitar, voice lessons, sea shanty singing, and brass-band tuba (Krewe de Noiz Brass Band) — plus two named personal build projects, **Flaming Tuba** (a fire-modified tuba, in progress) and **Heart Bike** (a communal art bike, maintained since at least 2016).

**Themes across the work:** rigorous technical craft in service of playful, public-facing art; teaching and empowerment as a recurring thread (freak-bike welding workshops, Helmahawks' kid-safety design); and a fifteen-year, still-active presence inside Bay Area fire-art and maker culture, most recently as Build Lead on a full sponsored installation.
