# Community Bio (draft v2, 2026-07-04) — for makerspace/co-op-housing introductions

*Distinct from the professional/Anthropic-facing bio: this version is meant for introducing Jay to a new makerspace, cooperative house, or intentional community — the audience wants to know she's "been around the block" and understands community-space culture, not that she's a PhD scientist. Third person; adapt to first person as needed for a self-introduction. Full research/sourcing for every item below lives in its own `experiences/` folder.*

---

Julia "Jay" Hasty — known in these circles as **"Jaybird"** — has been part of Bay Area maker, hacker, and cooperative-living culture for close to two decades. She's not new to shared space, consensus-driven governance, or the particular texture of community-run infrastructure — she's helped found it, rescue it from crisis, and keep it running. Across every one of these spaces, the same pattern shows up: when a community rejects top-down hierarchy, she's the one who steps up to do the structural work — writing policy, running committees, resolving conflict, being the legal signatory — that makes leaderless governance actually survive contact with reality. She calls this thread of her own life **"Community Building & Third Spaces."**

**Community/makerspace history:**

- **The Shipyard (Berkeley, pre-2009–2009)** — co-host, community-building role at this large-scale mechatronic-art warehouse, before it formalized into ALL Power Labs. Lived on-site as "Chemist-in-Residence"; beyond just living there, she hosted the after-hours social scene — the community-host role, not just a tenant.
- **Monkeyelectric (2009–2010)** — worked alongside founder Dan Goldwater's Bay Area LED/bike-art shop; part of the same maker-culture network that also produced The Shipyard and APL. Dan Goldwater personally taught her circuit-board design and LED programming.
- **East Bay Bike Party — co-founder, with Dan Goldwater.** A recurring, still-running community bike ride that's been going for many years (per Jay's own account — not yet independently re-confirmed with dates in this research pass).
- **Sudo Room / Omni Commons (Oakland, member for years)** — at a free hackerspace collective whose own mission is self-government and horizontal/consensus community structure.
- **Noisebridge (San Francisco, member)** — a "do-ocratic," consensus-run hackerspace with no formal leadership, motto "we teach, we learn, we share."
- **TeamHouse (San Juan, PR, 2018–2019, member and advisor)** — Entrepreneur-in-Residence and paying tenant at a curated, application-only co-living/co-working community built post-Hurricane Maria to cluster compatible founders together.
- **Bay Area MakerFarm (Dec 2020–present, co-founder)** — a real 501(c)(3), fiscally sponsored by Common Vision, on Alameda Point Collaborative land. Direct work in conflict resolution, policy-writing, and governance. Personally led the farm through a Dec 2020 COVID-era 60-day emergency relocation crisis after the prior makerspace lost its lease — found the new 1-acre plot, negotiated a below-market deal, hired a governance-structure facilitator, and helped execute a rain-soaked physical move of 15 shipping containers and farm animals. First rotating Board of Directors signatory (a pure legal-signatory role in a deliberately anarchist/egalitarian collective — not a hierarchical title, and should never be framed as one).
- **Shadetree (5th Avenue Point, Oakland)** — Jay's own words: "the successful cooperative housing project." A real, celebrated (KQED, NPR, Oakland Heritage Alliance award) affordable-housing preservation story — ~18-30 artist/tradesperson residents formed a nonprofit (SHADE) and raised $2.5-2.8M from 21+ individual lenders in 11 months to buy their building outright and avoid displacement by a condo developer (2016-2017 crisis, resolved). Jay was personally present during the crisis ("everyone over here is tense, hopefully we save shadetree," May 2017) and later became a resident (confirmed address 2023), still actively engaged as of 2026 (organizing community events, participating in the co-op's internal Slack). Full detail in `experiences/shadetree/README.md` — a few of her own specifics (exact role during the crisis, when she formally moved in) are still open questions for her to fill in.
- **Michael Christian's** (~Dec 2016–Mar 2017) — a paying live/work tenancy on the property of Michael Christian, a well-known large-scale Bay Area sculptor (20-time Burning Man arts grant recipient) — also a fellow ALL Power Labs shareholder, so this overlapped with her professional network too.
- **Flava Packet** (~2015–2016) — an informal Bay Area party/community house she was closely tied to (stored personal belongings there, used it as a Burning Man-prep home base).
- **Finn House** (~2023–2024) — a shared rental house in Oakland where she organized community dinners and split household bills/utilities with roommates.

**Governing philosophy, in Jay's own words:** she's a strong advocate for "3rd and even 4th spaces" — physical community infrastructure beyond home and work — and sees maker/hacker/cooperative culture as a genuine innovation engine, not just a hobby scene. She brings direct experience with consensus governance, crisis response under real time pressure for a community (not a company), and the specific challenge of keeping shared physical infrastructure running long-term — demonstrated twice over, at both Bay Area MakerFarm and Shadetree.
