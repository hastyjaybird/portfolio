# Professional Biography — Dr. Jay Hasty

*Long-form biography for general employment consideration. Written in the third person.*

---

Dr. Jay Hasty is a scientist, engineer, and entrepreneur whose career spans hands-on laboratory research, large-scale hardware engineering, international humanitarian deployment, energy-regulatory policy work, and independent technology consulting. She holds a PhD in Materials Science and Engineering (Chemical Engineering focus) from Stony Brook University (SUNY), and over the past two decades has moved fluidly between the lab, the field, and the boardroom — building physical systems that have been deployed in 35+ countries, winning the XPrize (2018), and advising both startups and a government energy regulator in an extremely complex technical and policy environment.

## Early Technical Foundations

Jay's technical path began in the laboratory. As an undergraduate researcher, she conducted chemistry research that led to two peer-reviewed publications: one on the coordination chemistry of antioxidant iron-binding compounds, and one on environmentally benign wood-preservative chemistry, both funded through a National Science Foundation Research Experience for Undergraduates (NSF-REU) program. During this period she also took part in community environmental-education efforts in the Appalachian region around raising awareness of the impacts of mountain-top removal coal mining and a solar installer jobs training and transition program for former coal miners.

After completing her undergraduate studies, she worked as a Lab Technician at the American Red Cross National Testing Laboratory in Portland, Oregon, performing FDA-regulated nucleic acid testing for HIV, Hepatitis C, and West Nile Virus in a high-throughput blood-screening facility. During her time there, she was selected for a global research team formed in response to an emerging prion-disease (Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease) blood-safety concern, tasked with preparing the laboratory for a new class of testing years before a licensed test for the disease existed — early experience contributing to a genuinely unsolved technical and public-health problem.

She also spent time as a Lead Researcher at the Windward Education & Research Center in the Pacific Northwest, where she helped run a biomass-to-methanol research program — including grid-tied solar installation, microcontroller-based process control, and grant writing — and did her first hands-on work with biomass gasification technology, an area that would come to define the next phase of her career. Around the same time, she completed a hands-on solar installer training course through Portland Community College, then worked briefly as a residential solar installation technician for SunBurst Solar, and served as Chief Technology Officer for Portland Biodiesel, believed to be among the largest 100% waste-vegetable-oil biodiesel facilities on the U.S. West Coast at the time, processing waste vegetable oil into transportation fuel at a rate of roughly one million gallons per year.

## All Power Labs — Engineering Leadership at Global Scale

Following the 2008 financial downturn, Jay relocated to Berkeley, California, and joined a small early-stage team at All Power Labs (APL) developing compact biomass gasification systems — technology that converts waste biomass into usable heat, power, and biochar. She was the team's only member with a formal STEM background, and personally carried the chemistry, chemical engineering, and materials science that made the underlying technology work.

Over roughly a decade, she rose from design engineer to Director of Engineering, ultimately directing a team of more than fifteen engineers and helping grow the company to approximately fifty employees across offices in the U.S., Italy, and the Philippines. Under her technical leadership, the company scaled its systems from 25kW to 150kW gasification systems and deployed more than 1,000+ installations in 35+ countries. She personally hired and directed both mechanical engineers and an in-house software/controls team building custom industrial control systems — a genuine hardware-and-software engineering leadership role. She is a named inventor on three US patents covering compact gasifier-generator architecture.

As the company's customer base grew, Jay identified that field support calls were pulling engineering staff away from core R&D. She proposed and built a dedicated Product Support department to APL's board, personally hired and trained a team of technicians, and built a full reliability-engineering discipline underneath it — including statistical failure-rate modeling, a structured failure-investigation process, and an in-house quality assurance and burn-in testing program that accumulated thousands of engine-hours of testing data before products reached customers.

She also served as Principal Investigator on a National Science Foundation Small Business Innovation Research (NSF-SBIR) grant for a feedstock-flexible gasifier design, and led a USAID-funded rural electrification project that brought power to 250+ homes and a school in Liberia during the West African Ebola outbreak. Separately, she led engineering work on a California Energy Commission (CEC)-funded project converting forest-fire mitigation waste into on-demand renewable energy, advancing the technology from early prototype to a field-tested, proven system.

## Graduate Research and the Water Abundance XPrize

While working in industry, Jay developed an independent interest in gas-to-liquids chemistry, eventually building her own experimental reactor prototype. This work led her to pursue a PhD at Stony Brook University under a faculty researcher and department co-chair, Devinder Mahajan, recognized internationally in nano-catalysis, studying nano-catalyst synthesis and characterization for the conversion of syngas into mixed alcohols. Arriving to find the laboratory newly established and largely without equipment, she personally received, installed, and commissioned the lab's core analytical and reactor infrastructure — including gas chromatography/mass spectrometry, high-performance liquid chromatography, and a full pilot-scale reactor suite spanning fixed-bed, slurry-phase, and fluidized-bed configurations. She also served as her research center's safety committee lead and later taught as an Adjunct Professor for Chemical Engineering Senior Design.

Her graduate research produced two peer-reviewed journal publications and roughly a dozen conference presentations, including an oral presentation at a COP21-affiliated workshop in Paris, France on agricultural greenhouse gas emissions.

In parallel with her graduate studies, Jay served as Director of Engineering for SkySource Alliance, a joint-venture spinoff of All Power Labs, leading the team that designed, integrated, tested, and validated a biomass-powered atmospheric water generation system. In 2018, that system won the $1.5 million Grand Prize in the international Water Abundance XPrize competition — a global competition for technology that generates clean drinking water from the atmosphere using renewable energy.

During this same period, she also served as COO / VP of Business Development for BoxPower, a separate mobile solar microgrid company, running that role concurrently with her XPrize engineering work through a deliberate, sprint-based approach to managing overlapping high-intensity projects. In that capacity she completed Launch Alaska, an intensive clean-energy business accelerator program focused on Alaska's uniquely complex energy market — isolated microgrids, extreme logistics, and diesel dependence — attending in person and completing the program in full. That experience directly preceded and enabled BoxPower's first microgrid installation in rural Alaska.

## Entrepreneurial Ventures in Early-Stage Technology Commercialization

Jay has twice served as Entrepreneurial Lead commercializing early-stage laboratory technology out of a university research group, working alongside the original inventing faculty researcher. In the first venture, NanoSulf, she led customer discovery for a nano-catalyst technology designed to remove hydrogen sulfide from landfill and biogas streams, personally conducting more than 100 interviews with landfill operators, engineering firms, and industry consultants, and building a full market segmentation, pricing, and go-to-market model across the landfill gas, wastewater, and oil-and-gas sectors.

In the second venture, CPWater Tech, she led commercialization efforts for a novel, chemical-free wastewater purification technology using a recyclable gas-hydrate process, again running rigorous customer discovery — more than 70 interviews inside real brewery and industrial facilities — while the underlying patent application was filed. Both ventures received National Science Foundation I-Corps recognition, and CPWater Tech also received a two-stage New York State clean-technology grant. Jay describes both experiences as equally valuable: her focus was always on rigorously testing whether real customers wanted what a laboratory had built, not simply validating a pitch deck.

## Puerto Rico: Humanitarian Response, Electric Cooperative Development, and Energy Policy

In the aftermath of Hurricane Maria, BoxPower contributed a containerized solar system to one of the hardest-hit communities on the island as a humanitarian gesture, and Jay personally traveled to install it over roughly ten days. While in Puerto Rico, she recognized a significant opportunity in newly passed regulation permitting electric utility cooperatives to form and operate on the island for the first time — and made the decision to stay and help build the resulting industry rather than return home.

She went on to co-found ACEPR, an accelerator program supporting the formation of electric utility cooperatives across Puerto Rico, providing training and mentorship in engineering design, regulatory compliance, financial modeling, and organizational structure to a cohort of emerging cooperative projects — among them a large hydroelectric-cooperative initiative and a solar-and-battery cooperative built for a rural, low-income mountain community that had endured an extended hurricane-related outage. As part of this work, she engaged directly with Puerto Rico's energy regulator, including regulatory lobbying and public-comment work to help streamline the certification process for a wave of new cooperative applicants.

Separately, she co-founded and led IOGS (Industrial Off-Grid Systems), developing an independent-power-producer model for a large industrial real-estate portfolio spanning more than fifty warehouse buildings and roughly 5 million square feet — representing a substantial share of all industrial real estate on the island — designing a multi-megawatt battery storage program sized for multi-day critical-load resilience and building the accompanying financial and tax-incentive structure. The project reached a fully engineered, construction-ready design stage.

These relationships ultimately led to her current, ongoing work as a direct technical and regulatory contractor to the Puerto Rico Energy Bureau (PREB), through a firm called MaxETA, where she has visibility across LUMA (the island's transmission and distribution operator), Genera (the generation operator), PREPA (the public utility), third-party aggregators, and electric cooperatives. Her work there has spanned emergency-response and physical- and cyber-security plan review, utility performance-metrics design, distribution planning, and — most recently — service on the drafting team for a major base-rate proceeding described as one of the most complex utility rate cases in the U.S.

## Independent Consulting Practice

In 2017, Jay founded Corvidae, LLC, an independent technical and business consulting practice, later co-founding a second firm, Resilient Markets, LLC, through which she has advised 15+ clean-technology clients and startups on technical feasibility, thermodynamics, market strategy, and go-to-market planning — spanning waste-to-fuel systems, hydrogen production, biochar, green solvents, battery technology, and distributed solar and wind generation. Her client work has included due diligence for Celeres, an investment firm evaluating clean-technology deals, engineering design consulting for Charm Industrial during its earliest, pre-pivot phase, and sustained technical and business-development support for Scarab Tech, a waste-to-fuel hardware company.

In one notable engagement, she helped SolSolutions, a small solar-equipment retailer, reposition its business away from a struggling storefront-retail model — which was losing ground to larger, better-financed competitors — toward larger commercial project work, later supporting the same client's capital-raise strategy and investor-facing materials.

Through this practice she has also supported New Power Industry, a distributed-manufacturing venture studio, across several of its portfolio efforts, including pitch and fundraising strategy, brand and market positioning work, and hands-on technical and design advisory during the development and field testing of BetterStove, a clean-burning biomass cookstove intended for underserved communities.

## Current Work

Jay currently serves as Technical Lead for 10Power, a tribal-energy consulting practice, where she authored a comprehensive energy plan for the Native Village of Eklutna, Alaska — covering biomass, solar, hydroelectric, and biochar options alongside food-sovereignty and carbon-market considerations — and continues an active technical collaboration with the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) on a follow-on community solar project.

She is also the independent developer of ClimateDash, a personal multi-agent AI research platform for climate-technology market analysis, built on a modern full-stack architecture with automated research agents, a knowledge graph, and human-in-the-loop review workflows — reflecting a hands-on, self-taught fluency with contemporary AI development tools that she has also used to mentor others, including helping a family member with decades of software engineering experience get up to speed on modern AI-assisted, API-driven development.

## Community Leadership and Civic Engagement

Outside her paid work, Jay has a long, consistent history of taking on the structural and organizational work that keeps grassroots, member-run communities and technical collectives functioning. She is a co-founder of Bay Area MakerFarm, a community makerspace and farm, where she helped design its governance structure, served as its first governing signatory, and led conflict-resolution and facilities work — including personally securing a new site and coordinating an emergency relocation when the organization lost its original lease with short notice. She has also been a long-standing member of two community-run Bay Area hackerspaces including Sudo Room / Omni Commonss, and served as a member and advisor at TeamHouse, a curated co-living community for entrepreneurs in Puerto Rico. Early in her career, while living on-site at The Shipyard, the informal workshop space that would eventually grow into All Power Labs, she played a similar connective role, including personally teaching welding and metal-lathe operation, and other fabrication skills to newcomers.

Jay also maintains an active volunteer record in disaster resilience and humanitarian energy access: she has volunteered extensively with La Finca de Hamberto a Puerto Rico-based community-resilience and regenerative-agriculture initiative. She also served as a technical reviewer and juror for the Land Art Generator Initiative's Fly Ranch renewable-energy public-art design competition, judged for Clean Tech Open competitions, and conducted a due-diligence and vetting trip on behalf of Sail Relief, a disaster-relief organization, evaluating a solar microgrid installation for an under-resourced school near Cape Town, South Africa. Her broader volunteer record also includes a wastewater-treatment support project with Terraqua Barranca in Peru, a clean-energy access initiative with Fenix International in Uganda, and a university graduate-led penguin research expedition with Earthwatch in Argentine Patagonia.

## Education, Publications, and Recognition

Jay holds a PhD in Materials Science and Engineering (Chemical Engineering focus) from Stony Brook University. She is the author or co-author of four peer-reviewed scientific publications spanning landfill gas management, nano-catalyst synthesis for syngas conversion, coordination chemistry, and wood-preservative chemistry, and has presented her work at numerous international conferences, including a United Nations (UN) climate conference-affiliated workshop in Paris. She is a named inventor on three U.S. patents for compact gasifier-generator architecture, and has received recognition including the Water Abundance XPrize Grand Prize (2018), National Science Foundation (NSF-SBIR) and I-Corps awards, a USAID rural electrification grant, and a New York State clean-technology commercialization award. She is a longtime member of the American Chemical Society, dating to her undergraduate studies, and has served on multiple technical advisory boards for early-stage clean-technology companies.

Across every chapter of her career — laboratory science, large-scale hardware engineering, humanitarian deployment, energy regulation, and independent consulting — Jay has consistently taken on the work of bridging rigorous technical development with the real-world needs of the people and communities a technology is meant to serve.
